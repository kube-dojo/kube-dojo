# People: Chapter 42 - CUDA

## Core Actors

- **Ian Buck** - Stanford PhD researcher, Brook development lead, and later NVIDIA GPU-compute/CUDA leader. NVIDIA's current bio says he joined NVIDIA in 2004 after completing his PhD and calls Brook the forerunner to generalized GPU computing; Buck told Tom's Hardware that he joined NVIDIA to start CUDA in 2005 and that the project began with him and one other engineer. Anchors: NVIDIA bio lines 5-8; Tom's Hardware lines 467-493. Date conflict: do not assert a single exact year without noting the conflict.
- **Pat Hanrahan** - Stanford graphics professor and Buck's coauthor on the 2003 data-parallel computation report and the 2004 Brook paper. Anchors: Stanford abstract lines 2-12; HGPU lines 44-75.
- **John Nickolls** - NVIDIA director of architecture for GPU computing and coauthor of the 2008 ACM Queue CUDA article; later bio in that article says his interests included parallel processing systems, languages, and architectures. Anchor: ACM Queue lines 134-135.
- **Michael Garland** - NVIDIA researcher and coauthor of the 2008 ACM Queue CUDA article. Anchor: ACM Queue author line 14 and bio lines 136-137.
- **Kevin Skadron** - University of Virginia professor and coauthor of the 2008 ACM Queue CUDA article; the article notes he was on sabbatical with NVIDIA Research. Anchor: ACM Queue author line 14 and bio lines 136-137.
- **Jensen Huang** - NVIDIA CEO. In this chapter he is relevant as the executive later reported to have marketed GPUs to supercomputing customers in 2006 and argued that CUDA could enlarge that market. Anchor: New Yorker lines 116-124. Keep motive claims careful.

## Supporting Actors and Institutions

- **NVIDIA GeForce 8800/G80 engineering team** - The technical brief attributes a summer-2002 start to the GeForce 8800 architecture design and names goals around performance, image quality, GPU physics/high-end floating point, and DirectX 10 features. Anchor: NVIDIA technical brief p.26, lines 496-514.
- **Stanford Graphics Lab / Stanford computer science** - Site of Buck's Brook work and the earlier GPGPU abstraction framing. Anchors: Stanford abstract lines 2-12; HGPU lines 44-75.
- **Early GPU-computing researchers and users** - Wired's 2006 Supercomputing coverage names UNC Chapel Hill GPU benchmarks and Stanford Folding@Home GPU experiments as part of the wider context. Anchor: Wired lines 80-89 and 129-145.
- **Ting-Wai Chiu** - Later New Yorker reporting presents Chiu as one of Huang's model early scientific-computing customers. Anchor: New Yorker lines 119-121. Treat as secondary retrospective color, not a causal proof.

## Boundary Notes

- Do not turn Buck into the sole inventor of every CUDA feature. The verified sources support his central role and the Brook-to-CUDA bridge, but the hardware architecture and software stack were NVIDIA team products.
- Do not use Jensen Huang as a viewpoint character unless the sentence is grounded in a source. The chapter can say later reporting describes his supercomputing push; it cannot invent his internal reasoning.
- Do not introduce Alex Krizhevsky, Geoffrey Hinton, Ilya Sutskever, or AlexNet as active Chapter 42 characters except in a final "see Ch43" sentence.

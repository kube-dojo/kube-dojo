# People: Chapter 24 - The Math That Waited for the Machine

## Verified Protagonists

- **David E. Rumelhart:** Cognitive scientist and PDP leader. Central to framing neural networks as models that learn internal representations rather than hand-coded symbols.
- **Geoffrey E. Hinton:** Neural-network researcher who helped make representation learning the central claim of the 1986 backpropagation revival. Important conflict note: do not present him as sole inventor.
- **Ronald J. Williams:** Co-author of the 1986 Nature and PDP backpropagation papers; essential to the algorithmic presentation and error-propagation work.
- **Paul J. Werbos:** Earlier researcher whose 1974/1975 thesis connected backward derivative propagation to adaptive prediction systems. Needed to keep the invention story honest.
- **Seppo Linnainmaa:** Automatic-differentiation researcher associated with reverse accumulation before the neural-network revival. Useful for showing that the derivative machinery had a separate mathematical lineage.
- **James L. McClelland:** PDP co-editor and connectionist collaborator; context figure for why 1986 landed as a cognitive-science movement rather than only an optimization paper.
- **Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert:** Background antagonists from the perceptron limitation story. They belong primarily to Ch17, but their critique sets the frozen-hidden-layer problem for this chapter.

## Review Notes

- Avoid "godfather" language and single-hero framing.
- Attribute the 1986 impact to a working synthesis: algorithm, demonstrations, PDP framing, and available simulation infrastructure.

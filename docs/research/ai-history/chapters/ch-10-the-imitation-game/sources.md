## Conflict Notes

- **Mind volume number** — see `brief.md` Conflict Notes. Standard citation is Mind 59 (236); the verification PDF's header reading "Mind 49" is a transcription error.
- **"Imitation Game" vs "Turing Test"** — Turing called the experiment the Imitation Game; the rebrand "Turing Test" was applied by later commentators. The chapter preserves Turing's term in scenes 1-4 and uses "Turing Test" only in Scene 5 where the rebrand is itself the topic.
- **Gender-imitation: original or vestigial?** — see `brief.md` Conflict Notes. The chapter acknowledges the Proudfoot 2011 vs. abstracted-test disagreement without picking a side, and does not bury Mind p. 433's gender-disambiguation setup.
- **The 1948 chess experiment as proto-imitation game** — see `brief.md` Conflict Notes. Presented as the *operational shape* of the test arriving early, not as a single-author priority dispute.

## Notes on dating

- The conference titles 6th/7th/8th sometimes drift between sources because the published volumes confusingly renumber after the renaming. The Pias 2003 edition fixes the canonical numbering: ten conferences total, 1946-1953, with the first stenographic record at the sixth (March 1949). The chapter should use the Pias numbering.
- The "summer 1947" coining of "cybernetics" is dated by Wiener himself (1961 Introduction p. 11). Some secondary sources push it earlier (to 1946 or even 1944) on the basis of pre-publication discussions; Wiener's own dating is the safer anchor.
- The AA-predictor's failure date is best framed as "December 1942 / January 1943" rather than a single point — Galison shows Wiener compiling the hits-comparison data "[in] December 1942 and January 1943" (p. 244) and writing the resignation letter to Weaver early in 1943.

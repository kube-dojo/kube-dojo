# Brief: Chapter 6 - The Cybernetics Movement

## Thesis
Before AI became synonymous with software, the Cybernetics movement defined intelligence as a continuous, physical system of homeostasis and negative feedback loops, heavily inspired by analog systems.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Norbert Wiener, Arturo Rosenblueth, Julian Bigelow. The 1943 paper on teleology, the 1948 book *Cybernetics*, the Macy Conferences.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Digital symbolic logic.

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Anti-Aircraft Gun:** Wiener and Bigelow's WWII work modeling predictive feedback loops.
2. **Purpose and Teleology:** The 1943 realization that purpose is just a mechanism of feedback.
3. **The Macy Conferences:** Interdisciplinary debates establishing the cybernetic paradigm.

# Brief: Chapter 6 - The Cybernetics Movement

## Thesis
Before AI became synonymous with software, the Cybernetics movement defined intelligence as a continuous, physical system of homeostasis and negative feedback loops, heavily inspired by analog systems.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Norbert Wiener's anti-aircraft predictor (1940s), the 1943 Rosenblueth/Wiener/Bigelow paper on teleology, the Macy Conferences (1946-1953) with Mead, Bateson, McCulloch, and von Neumann, W. Ross Ashby's Homeostat (1948).
- OUT OF SCOPE: Grey Walter's Tortoises (next chapter).

## Scenes Outline
1. **Tracking the Target:** Wiener and Bigelow's WWII work modeling predictive feedback loops, narrated via Wiener's own recollections.
2. **Purpose and Teleology:** The 1943 realization that purpose is just a mechanism of feedback.
3. **The Macy Conferences:** Interdisciplinary debates establishing the cybernetic paradigm, featuring verbatim transcripts from the Beekman Hotel.
4. **Design for a Brain:** W. Ross Ashby and the Homeostat, expanding homeostasis beyond the thermostat metaphor.

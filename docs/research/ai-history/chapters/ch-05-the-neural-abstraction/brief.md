# Brief: Chapter 5 - The Neural Abstraction

## Thesis
McCulloch and Pitts created the first idealized mathematical model of artificial neurons. By treating biological nervous activity as a logical calculus, they provided the theoretical foundation for neural networks, though their lack of a learning mechanism directly set up the next decade of research.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Walter Pitts's background, Russell's encouragement, the Chicago school formation, Carnap's logical syntax, the 1943 paper, the no-learning limitation (Hebb 1949).
- OUT OF SCOPE: Literal biological equivalence; The Perceptron (Chapter 14).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Vagabond Genius:** Pitts's background, Russell's encouragement, and meeting McCulloch by 1942.
2. **The Chicago School:** The intellectual circle forming around Wiener, McCulloch, Pitts, and Lettvin.
3. **The Idealized Threshold:** Pedagogical reproduction of the McCulloch-Pitts equations using Carnap's logical syntax.
4. **The Unified Theory:** The 1943 paper bridging logical calculus and idealized nervous activity.
5. **The No-Learning Limitation:** The model was hard-coded, leaving the problem of learning to Donald Hebb (1949).

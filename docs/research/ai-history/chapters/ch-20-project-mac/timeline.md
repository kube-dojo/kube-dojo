# Timeline: Chapter 20 - Project MAC

- 1961: CTSS is demonstrated at the MIT Computation Center; this predates
  Project MAC and should be treated as a root, not a MAC invention.
- Spring 1963: Project MAC is organized at MIT for Machine-Aided Cognition and
  Multiple-Access Computer systems under ONR acting for ARPA.
- Early 1963: Norberg/O'Neill describe DARPA approval of a $3-million Project
  MAC contract after Licklider's direct involvement in the proposal and review.
- July 1, 1963: MIT's MAC50 retrospective gives this as Project MAC's founding
  date.
- Fall 1963: a substantially improved CTSS installation is at Project MAC,
  according to Fano's 1967 account.
- 1964-1965: Project MAC's IBM 7094 CTSS work continues, while Multics begins
  as a second-generation time-sharing project with Bell Labs and GE.
- 1967: Fano publishes "The Computer Utility and the Community," framing
  time-sharing as a social and intellectual utility, not just scheduling.
- 1968-1969: the AI group reports its PDP-6/PDP-10 ITS environment, MATHLAB,
  MACLISP, real-time control, mechanical hands, and computer eyes.
- 1970-1971: Project MAC reports ARPA/IPTO support, Multics acceptance, ARPANET
  protocol work, and MACSYMA's maturity.
- 1972-1973: Project MAC is reorganized into four divisions; Honeywell's
  Multics product announcement marks the end of Project MAC's development role
  in Multics; MACSYMA and network usage expand.
- Early 1970s: Project MAC splits into the MIT AI Lab and Laboratory for
  Computer Science, according to MIT Museum's subject summary.
- 2003: LCS and the AI Lab merge into CSAIL; keep this as a brief lineage note,
  not the body of the chapter.

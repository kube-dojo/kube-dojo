# Timeline: Chapter 16 - The Cold War Blank Check

| Date | Event | Status / Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| 1950-1956 | RAND, GSIA, ONR, Air Force, and MIT military-lab channels support early symbolic-AI-adjacent work before ARPA/IPTO exists. | Green - NRC 1999 pp.201-204 ([G07]-[G09]). |
| Summer 1956 | Newell, Simon, and Shaw bring Logic Theorist into the Dartmouth moment; its RAND/JOHNNIAC implementation becomes an early proof that symbolic reasoning can run on a digital machine. | Green for RAND/JOHNNIAC funding context - NRC 1999 pp.201-203 ([G07]); Green for Newell 1957 bibliographic abstract ([G10]). |
| September 1957 | McCarthy and Minsky establish the MIT Artificial Intelligence Project under modest RLE support. | Green - NRC 1999 p.203 ([G09]). |
| October 4, 1957 | Sputnik launch shocks U.S. national-security planners. | Green - DARPA Sputnik Surprise section ([G01]). |
| February 7, 1958 | ARPA is created in the Sputnik aftermath. | Green - DARPA Sputnik Surprise section ([G01]). |
| March 1960 | Licklider publishes "Man-Computer Symbiosis," naming time-sharing, memory, programming languages, and input/output equipment as prerequisites for close human-computer cooperation. | Green - Licklider 1960 Summary / Sections 1.1, 5.2 ([G03]). |
| June-November 1961 | DoD/IDA command-and-control studies identify computer-related research areas that lead toward IPTO. | Green - Kita 2003 pp.62-63 ([G04]). |
| October 1962 | Licklider begins as ARPA/IPTO director. | Green - Norberg/O'Neill 1996 p.29; Kita 2003 p.62 ([G04], [G05]). |
| 1962-1963 | IPTO and SDC redirect the Q-32 toward time-sharing, and Stanford/McCarthy's LISP work is tied to the SDC Q-32 rather than a new Stanford time-sharing machine. | Green - Norberg/O'Neill pp.102-103; McCarthy "Beyond LISP 1.5" ([G17], [G22]). |
| April 1963 | Licklider addresses the "Members and Affiliates of the Intergalactic Computer Network" memo to IPTO-funded researchers. | Green - Kita 2003 p.65; specific original memo scan still needed for direct quotation. |
| July 1, 1963 | Project MAC begins at MIT as a major IPTO-supported experiment in interactive, time-shared computing. | Green - DARPA Project MAC entry; MIT MAC50; Project MAC PR II p.xiii ([G14], [G19]). |
| June 15, 1963 | Stanford's AI Project begins ARPA-supported work according to Stanford's 1973 AI Lab report. | Green - Stanford CS-TR-74-409 pp.1-2 ([G18]). |
| 1964-1965 | Project MAC reports its broad online-computing goal and CTSS/Multics infrastructure; CTSS is both service facility and man-machine-interaction laboratory. | Green - Project MAC PR II p.xiii, p.63 ([G19], [G20]). |
| 1965 | IPTO program areas include command-and-control processes, human-machine interaction, computer languages, programming techniques, time sharing, and advanced software. | Green - Norberg/O'Neill pp.44-45 ([G23]). |
| 1965-1966 | Stanford expands the AI Project, orders a PDP-6, and moves to the D.C. Power Laboratory. | Green - Stanford CS-TR-74-409 pp.1-2 ([G18]). |
| 1966 | SRI's AI Center emerges, focused on automata for hostile environments, leading to Shakey-related research. | Yellow - NRC 1999 pp.204-205 summarizes Nilsson 1984; original contract/report needed ([Y04]). |
| 1968 | Edwards reports that ARPA budgets begin including AI as a separate line item. | Green as Edwards interpretation - *The Closed World* Ch.8 p.273 ([G28]). |
| 1968-1969 | MIT Project MAC's AI section documents PDP-6/PDP-10/ITS, real-time device control, MATHLAB, and MACLISP. | Green - Project MAC PR VI pp.11-53 to 11-58 ([G21]). |
| November 19, 1969 | Public Law 91-121 Sec.203 becomes law, restricting FY1970 defense research funds to projects with direct and apparent military-function relationships. | Green - 83 Stat.206 ([G25]). |
| June 23, 1970 | GAO reports on DoD implementation of Sec.203 and notes unresolved disposition of equipment tied to disqualified research projects. | Green - GAO B-167034 Highlights ([G26]). |
| FY1971 | Norberg/O'Neill table shows "Intelligent systems," "Speech understanding systems," "Distributed information systems," and "Computer networks" among IPTO program areas. | Green - Norberg/O'Neill pp.44-45 ([G23]). |
| July 1973 | Stanford's ten-year AI report says the lab had 128 members, with about two-thirds at least partly ARPA-supported. | Green - Stanford CS-TR-74-409 pp.1-2 ([G18]). |

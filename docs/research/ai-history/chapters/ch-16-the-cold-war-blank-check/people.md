# People: Chapter 16 - The Cold War Blank Check

## Funding / Program Actors

- **J. C. R. Licklider** - First IPTO director and central figure in the chapter. Anchors: Licklider 1960 for the symbiosis vision ([G03]); Norberg/O'Neill pp.29-30 for funding criteria and $11M combined IPTO/Behavioral Sciences budget ([G05], [G06]); Kita pp.62-73 for command/control and thinking-center interpretation.
- **Jack Ruina** - ARPA director who hired Licklider and approved the Project MAC contract per Norberg/O'Neill. Anchor: Norberg/O'Neill pp.29-30 and pp.98-100 ([G05], [G15]).
- **Ivan Sutherland** - Licklider's successor at IPTO; relevant as the bridge from time-sharing to graphics and man-machine communication. Anchor: Kita pp.71-73. Keep concise; Ch16 is not a Sketchpad chapter.
- **Robert Taylor** - Later IPTO director who continued Licklider's center/funding philosophy. Anchor: Norberg/O'Neill pp.30-31. Use only if the prose needs continuity beyond Licklider.
- **Lawrence Roberts** - Later IPTO director; important for ARPANET and SUR but mostly forward reference here. Anchor: NRC 1999 pp.205-206 for SUR; ARPANET belongs to Ch37.
- **Senator Mike Mansfield** - Sponsor associated with the 1969 restriction on DoD research funding. Anchor: Public Law 91-121 Sec.203 ([G25]); do not infer motive beyond sources.

## AI Researchers / Center Builders

- **Allen Newell** - RAND/Carnegie AI researcher; Logic Theorist and GPS anchor early government-supported symbolic AI. Anchors: NRC pp.201-204 ([G07], [G08]); Newell 1957 RAND paper ([G10]).
- **Herbert A. Simon** - GSIA/RAND/Carnegie collaborator; his decision-making and problem-solving work ties behavioral science, RAND, and AI. Anchors: NRC pp.201-204 ([G07], [G08]).
- **J. C. Shaw** - RAND programmer on Logic Theorist; include when naming the actual implementation team. Anchor: NRC pp.201-203 ([G07]).
- **John McCarthy** - MIT AI Project co-founder and Stanford AI Project founder; central to LISP, Stanford, and Q-32/PDP-6 infrastructure. Anchors: NRC pp.203-205 ([G09], [G13]); Norberg/O'Neill p.103 ([G17]); McCarthy "Beyond LISP 1.5" ([G22]); Stanford CS-TR-74-409 pp.1-2 ([G18]).
- **Marvin Minsky** - MIT AI Project co-founder and Project MAC AI figure. Anchors: NRC p.203 ([G09]); Project MAC PR VI AI section pp.11-53 to 11-58 ([G21]).
- **Fernando Corbato and Robert Fano** - MIT time-sharing / Project MAC leaders. They matter as infrastructure builders rather than AI theorists. Anchors: Project MAC PR II p.xiii/p.63 ([G19], [G20]); Norberg/O'Neill pp.98-100 ([G15]).
- **Alan Perlis and Herbert Green** - Carnegie figures named by Norberg/O'Neill as part of the strength of the Carnegie proposal alongside Newell and Simon. Anchor: Norberg/O'Neill pp.102-103 ([G16]).
- **Edward Feigenbaum** - Stanford AI / Heuristic Dendral figure; relevant as part of Stanford growth and later expert-system line. Anchor: Stanford CS-TR-74-409 pp.1-2; keep details for later chapters.
- **Charles Rosen** - SRI AI Center leader; use for the SRI/Shakey scene only if prose stays cautious. Anchor: NRC pp.204-205 is Yellow for original contract specifics ([Y04]).
- **Frank Rosenblatt / ONR/RADC figures** - Only a contrast point for non-symbolic support and Ch17 handoff. Anchor: ONR Digital Computer Newsletter in Ch17 ([Y06] in Ch16).

- **SRI AI Center** - Later center tied to Shakey, STRIPS, vision/planning. Anchor: NRC pp.204-205, Yellow until original SRI reports are pulled ([Y04]).

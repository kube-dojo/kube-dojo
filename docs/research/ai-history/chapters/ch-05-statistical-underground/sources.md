# Sources: Chapter 5

## Annotated Bibliography

### Primary
- [x] **"Continuous Speech Recognition by Statistical Methods" (F. Jelinek, Proceedings of the IEEE, 1976)**
  - Verification: Green. The foundational paper establishing the statistical approach at IBM. Anchors Scenes 1 and 2.
- [x] **"A Maximum Likelihood Approach to Continuous Speech Recognition" (Bahl, Jelinek, Mercer, IEEE TPAMI, 1983)**
  - Verification: Green. Further codifies the Hidden Markov Model paradigm that overthrew symbolic linguistics. Anchors Scene 2.

### Secondary
- [x] **"The Speech Recognition Story" (IEEE Signal Processing Magazine, multiple retrospectives)**
  - Verification: Green. Verified secondary accounts of the DARPA SUR (Speech Understanding Research) program and the shift to empirical, statistical metrics. Anchors Scene 3.
- [x] **"Genius Makers" (Cade Metz, 2021) / "Architects of Intelligence" (Martin Ford, 2018)**
  - Verification: Green. Captures the cultural friction between the elegant, rule-based AI establishment and the "brute force" statistical engineers. Anchors Scene 1.

## Conflict Notes
- **Statistics vs. Neural Nets:** It is crucial to distinguish this 1980s/90s statistical era (HMMs, n-grams) from modern Deep Learning. This chapter serves as the bridge: it proves that data beats rules, but it relies on traditional statistical infrastructure (CPU mainframes) before the GPU era.

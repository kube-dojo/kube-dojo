# People: Chapter 33 — Deep Blue

Each entry is anchored to at least one verified primary or page-anchored secondary source. "Role in chapter" identifies which scenes the person carries.

## The IBM Deep Blue Team

### Feng-hsiung Hsu — chip designer; project lead from CMU

- **Born** Taiwan, 1959. **Education:** B.S. Electrical Engineering, National Taiwan University (1980); Ph.D. Computer Science, Carnegie Mellon University (1989). Doctoral thesis: *Large-Scale Parallelization of Alpha-Beta Search: An Algorithmic and Architectural Study*, CMU CSD, February 1990. (Hsu 1999 IEEE Micro p.81 author bio.)
- **Role:** Originator of the chess move generator that defines the entire ChipTest → Deep Thought → Deep Blue lineage. Chip architect for both Deep Blue I (1996) and Deep Blue II (1997). At IBM, member of the Watson Research Center staff; mailing address per Hsu 1999 p.81: "IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, MS 27:233, PO Box 218, Yorktown Heights, NY 10598."
- **Role in chapter:** Carries Scene 1 (CMU origins, IBM hire, 1996 match learning); load-bearing voice for Scene 2 (the chip and the cabinet) via his 1999 IEEE Micro paper.
- **Note:** Hsu 1999 p.81 closes with Hsu announcing his intent to form an "independent start-up to create a new chess chip for consumers" — useful context for why IBM disbanded the team after the rematch (Greenemeier 2017 SciAm Campbell quote: "we had achieved our goal"). The startup direction is not load-bearing for the chapter but explains the post-1997 dispersal.

### Murray S. Campbell — AI lead; primary post-match spokesperson

- **Education:** Doctoral student at Carnegie Mellon University; classmate of Hsu's. (Greenemeier 2017 SciAm — Campbell joined IBM late 1989.)
- **Role:** Co-author of Deep Thought (CMU); IBM Research staff from late 1989; author of post-match technical papers (Campbell-Hoane-Hsu AAAI SS-99-07 1999; Campbell, "Knowledge Discovery in Deep Blue," CACM 1999; Campbell-Hoane-Hsu, *Artificial Intelligence* 134, 2002). Was at the board for moves 40+ in Game 1 (Newborn 2003 p.150). Campbell's quoted retrospective in Newborn 2003 p.151 is the load-bearing source for the move-44-bug-as-psychological-cause framing.
- **Role in chapter:** Primary witness voice for Scene 3 (Game 1 bug + psychological aftermath) and the closing argument in Scene 5 (the team "had achieved our goal").
- **Current (per Greenemeier 2017):** "Distinguished research staff member in the AI Foundations group within IBM T. J. Watson Research Center's Cognitive Computing organization."

### A. Joseph Hoane Jr. — search-software lead; co-author

- **Role:** IBM Research; co-author on Hsu 1999 (acknowledgment p.81), AAAI SS-99-07 1999, and *Artificial Intelligence* 134 (2002). Worked the overnight bug-fix session after Game 1 (Newborn 2003 p.150 — "the logs confirmed the presence of the bug, and that evening Hoane worked to eliminate it"). Photographed with Kasparov during the 1997 rematch in IBM's "history" page.
- **Role in chapter:** Background presence in Scene 1 and Scene 3 (the overnight debug); not load-bearing for any individual quoted claim.

### Joel Benjamin — grandmaster consultant

- **Role:** US Chess Champion (multiple times); IBM consultant from late 1996 through the rematch. Newborn 2003 explicitly lists him as a Deep Blue team member at p.123 and his work appears throughout Chs. 8-13. Greenemeier 2017 SciAm Campbell interview describes Benjamin's two-part role: opening-library curation, and sparring partner who probed Deep Blue's weaknesses.
- **Role in chapter:** Carries the "Deep Blue's chess knowledge was tuned by a human grandmaster" boundary-contract beat in Scene 2; cited in Newborn 2003 p.160 reporting on Game 2's 41st-move analysis (e.g., that Deep Blue calculated Kasparov should have played 41...Kh7 instead of 41...Kf7).

### Other team members (background only — listed because contemporary sources name them)

- **C. J. Tan** — Deep Blue project manager; Newborn 2003 attributes contemporary statements to him at the post-Game-2 commentary auditorium (p.161). His "play like a grandmaster" quote appears in IBM corporate history.
- **Jerry Brody** — IBM team member (background; Newborn 2003 p.123); per Newborn 2003 p.221, retired after 15 years of service.
- **Thomas Anantharaman** — co-author with Hsu and Campbell on Deep Thought at CMU (Greenemeier 2017 SciAm). Not load-bearing for the chapter beyond establishing the CMU genealogy.
- **Matt Thoennes** — engineer; Newborn 2003 p.122 — drove the chip cards 50 miles between Yorktown Heights and Poughkeepsie integrating the SP2 system.

## The Human Opponent

### Garry Kasparov — World Chess Champion

- **Role:** Reigning Classical World Chess Champion 1985-2000. Played the 1996 Philadelphia match (won 4-2) and the 1997 New York rematch (lost 2.5-3.5).
- **In-chapter actions:**
  - Game 1 (5/3): wins; team analyses Deep Blue's 44th move and concludes Deep Blue must have searched 30-40 plies and seen everything lose (Newborn 2003 p.151, Campbell quote).
  - Game 2 (5/4): resigns in a position later shown drawable (Wikipedia citing Pandolfini and Friedel; Newborn 2003 Ch.11). Accuses IBM of cheating (Wikipedia citing the *Game Over* film and ChessBase 2015).
  - Game 6 (5/11): plays the Caro-Kann Defence in a deliberate attempt to push Deep Blue out of its opening-book preparation; resigns after 19.c4 in fewer than 20 moves (Wikipedia game record; Newborn 2003 Ch.13).
- **Rating:** ~2815 ELO during the rematch (Hsu 1999 IEEE Micro p.80).
- **Post-match published positions:** "IBM Owes Me a Rematch," *Time* 5/26/1997, pp. 38-39 (Newborn 2003 ref [7]). "I have to pay tribute," he said at the post-match press event; "the computer is far stronger than anybody expected." (IBM corporate history.)

## Witnesses, Commentators, Chroniclers

### Maurice Ashley — match commentator

- Provided live commentary at the rematch from the commentary auditorium at the Equitable Center; Newborn 2003 quotes Ashley directly at the start of his Ch.13 narration of Game 6 (p.187).

### Yasser Seirawan — four-time US chess champion; commentator

- Provided post-match analysis (Wikipedia game records + Newborn 2003 Game 1 / Game 2 commentary references). His direct *Wired* (2001) characterization of Deep Blue's 36.axb5 as "an incredibly refined move … sent Garry into a tizzy" is widely quoted in secondary sources but its primary anchor (the *Wired* 2001 article) is Yellow until directly verified.

### Frederic Friedel — ChessBase founder; commentator

- Co-managed live commentary; quoted at Newborn 2003 p.151 in the Game 1 narrative (concluding observations on Kasparov's first-game play with Fritz4).

### Bruce Weber — *New York Times* technology reporter

- Reported the engineering account of Deep Blue's Game 2 36th move on May 13, 1997. His article is the documentary primary for the "panic-time search extension" framing; Newborn 2003 pp.159-160 quotes and paraphrases it.

### Monty Newborn — chronicler

- Professor of Computer Science at McGill University; veteran of computer-chess organizing (was on the floor of the rematch). Author of the chapter's primary chronicle source: *Deep Blue: An Artificial Intelligence Milestone* (Springer-Verlag, 2003). Wrote an earlier book, *Kasparov Versus Deep Blue: Computer Chess Comes of Age* (Springer-Verlag, 1997), referenced internally by the 2003 book.

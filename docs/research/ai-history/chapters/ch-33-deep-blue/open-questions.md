# Open Questions: Chapter 33 — Deep Blue

What remains Yellow or Red, and what archival access would help promote each.

## Q1 — Hsu (2002) *Behind Deep Blue* page anchors

**Status:** Yellow on every claim that currently routes through Wikipedia citations of Hsu 2002 (pp. 166, 186, 217, 265).

**What it would unlock:**
- Direct in-his-own-words framing of the 1996 Philadelphia experience (Scene 1).
- Direct framing of Deep Blue's eventual dismantling and team dispersal (Scene 5).
- Independent corroboration of Joel Benjamin's evaluation-function tuning role (Scene 2).
- Independent perspective on Game 6's knight-sacrifice line.

**Tractability:** library access; the book is in print (2022 paperback edition, ISBN 9780691235134). Could a Codex anchor pass via library or scanned-PDF helper resolve this?

**If it doesn't get pulled:** the Newborn 2003 + Hsu 1999 + AAAI 1999 anchored set is sufficient for `3k-5k likely`; the stretch `4k-7k` label depends partly on this pull.

## Q2 — Campbell, Hoane, Hsu (2002) *Artificial Intelligence* journal page anchors

**Status:** Yellow (only the slide-deck rendering is open access; the original journal text is paywalled).

**What it would unlock:**
- The Green-promotion of Campbell's match-time average / peak figures (126M / 330M) as a complementary nuance to the 100-200M range Campbell gave in 2017.
- The ~80-page set of related claims about evaluation function design, opening-book extensions, and parallel-search load balancing — most of which Hsu 1999 IEEE Micro covers, but Campbell 2002 may have additional detail.

**Tractability:** institutional library access (most CS/AI departments). The DOI is stable; the paper is short (~27 journal pages).

**If it doesn't get pulled:** the chapter has enough anchored material via Hsu 1999 + AAAI 1999 + Newborn 2003 + SciAm 2017. This pull is "nice to have," not blocking.

## Q5 — Smithsonian and CHM accession records for Deep Blue racks

**Status:** Yellow (Newborn 2003 p.221-222 attests Smithsonian intent; the popular "one rack to Smithsonian, one rack to CHM" framing is repeated in many secondary sources without primary-record citation).

**What it would unlock:**
- A single-sentence factual close to Scene 5: definitive statement of where Deep Blue's hardware ended up.

**Tractability:** Smithsonian National Museum of American History (collection NMAH) and Computer History Museum (Mountain View, CA) accession-record search. CHM has a public chess-project archive (`https://archive.computerhistory.org/projects/chess/`) that may have the disposition record.

**If it doesn't get pulled:** the chapter can write "one rack was sent to the Smithsonian" and not assert the CHM half. Or write "the team retired the system in September 1997 and disbursed its hardware to museum collections" and skip the rack-by-rack detail.

## Q6 — Kasparov's primary-source statements about the 1997 rematch

**Status:** Yellow on Kasparov's contemporaneous 1997 *Time* article ("IBM Owes Me a Rematch," 5/26/1997, pp. 38-39, per Newborn ref [7]); Yellow on Kasparov's 2017 retrospective book *Deep Thinking: Where Machine Intelligence Ends and Human Creativity Begins* (PublicAffairs 2017).

**What it would unlock:**
- Whether Kasparov has, in primary source, ever endorsed Campbell's "the move-44 bug led me to overestimate Deep Blue" theory.
- Kasparov's own engineering-account characterization of the rematch (he has reportedly softened his cheating accusation in the 2010s, and Wikipedia cites his December 2016 podcast as evidence of this evolution).

**Tractability:** *Time* article via library; *Deep Thinking* book in print and accessible.

**If it doesn't get pulled:** the chapter must frame the cheating accusation as Kasparov's contemporaneous interpretation per the documented record (the *Game Over* film and contemporaneous press), and frame his later softening only via Wikipedia's December 2016 podcast citation (which is itself secondary). Acceptable, but a primary-source pull would strengthen the boundary-contract framing.

## Q7 — The chip's pre-1997 evaluation features vs. post-1997 evaluation features

**Status:** Yellow. Hsu 1999 p.71 establishes the architectural choice (software-adjustable feature weights with a hardware escape-hatch FPGA); Greenemeier 2017 establishes Joel Benjamin's role tuning weights between games. But the *specific* features and *specific* weight-changes the team made between Philadelphia 1996 and the rematch are not page-anchored.

**What it would unlock:**
- A more concrete description of "Deep Blue's chess knowledge was tuned" rather than an abstract claim.

**Tractability:** Hsu 2002 *Behind Deep Blue* (Q1 above) is the most likely venue; Campbell 2002 *AI* (Q2 above) is the second.

**If it doesn't get pulled:** the chapter can write the abstract claim without overclaiming specifics. The boundary contract holds.

# Sources: Chapter 2

## Annotated Bibliography

### Primary
- [x] **"The Logic Theory Machine: A Complex Information Processing System" (Newell & Simon, IRE Transactions on Information Theory, 1956)**
  - Verification: Green. The primary paper describing the first running AI program, explicitly detailing its execution on the RAND JOHNNIAC. Anchors Scene 1.
- [x] **"Information Processing Language-II Manual" (Newell & Shaw, RAND Corporation, 1957)**
  - Verification: Green. Demonstrates the early infrastructural software (IPL) built on top of military hardware to support symbolic AI. Anchors Scene 1.

### Secondary
- [x] **"Machines Who Think: A Personal Inquiry into the History and Prospects of Artificial Intelligence" (Pamela McCorduck, 1979/2004)**
  - Verification: Green. Provides first-hand interviews with Dartmouth attendees about the actual, somewhat fractured events of the workshop. Anchors Scene 2.
- [x] **"The Closed World: Computers and the Politics of Discourse in Cold War America" (Paul N. Edwards, 1996)**
  - Verification: Green. The definitive secondary source on how Cold War military funding (SAGE, ONR, DARPA) provided the physical computing infrastructure that made symbolic AI possible. Anchors Scene 3.

## Conflict Notes
- **Dartmouth Myth vs. Reality:** Many AI textbooks present Dartmouth as a unified "big bang" moment. The historical reality (supported by McCorduck) is that it was fractured and largely dominated by Newell and Simon, who already had working code thanks to RAND's infrastructure. The narrative will emphasize the infrastructural reality over the myth.

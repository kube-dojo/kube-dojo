# Timeline: Chapter 8

- **2014:** Sutskever et al. publish the Sequence to Sequence learning paper, establishing RNNs/LSTMs as the state-of-the-art for language tasks, but cementing a sequential bottleneck.
- **2015:** Google deploys the first Tensor Processing Units (TPU v1) internally, heavily optimized for matrix multiplication.
- **2017:**
  - June: The "Attention Is All You Need" paper is published by Google researchers, introducing the Transformer.
  - Google publishes the performance analysis of the TPU, making public the hardware advantages of matrix-heavy operations.
- **2018:** Google releases BERT, demonstrating that Transformers can be scaled massively on unlabeled text.
- **2020:** OpenAI publishes the "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models" paper, formalizing the relationship between compute, dataset size, and Transformer performance.

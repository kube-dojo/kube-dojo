# Brief: Chapter 8 - The Stored Program

## Thesis
The architectural separation of processing and memory, and the realization that instructions could be stored as data, bypassed the hardware rewiring bottleneck. This "von Neumann architecture" (built on the work of Eckert, Mauchly, and the ENIAC programmers) created the blank slate required for software.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: The ENIAC's physical cabling, the ENIAC programmers, J. Presper Eckert, John Mauchly, Herman Goldstine, John von Neumann, the EDVAC report, the patent disputes.
- OUT OF SCOPE: High-level programming languages (e.g., LISP).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Ballistics Bottleneck:** The ENIAC programmers (women mathematicians) physically route cables to "program" the machine.
2. **The EDVAC Draft:** Von Neumann synthesizes the ideas of the Moore School team into a formal report, omitting the names of Eckert and Mauchly.
3. **The Credit Dispute:** The fallout over the EDVAC report and the cementing of the "von Neumann architecture" moniker despite its collaborative origins.

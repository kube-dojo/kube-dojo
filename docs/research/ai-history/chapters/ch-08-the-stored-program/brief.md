# Brief: Chapter 8 - The Stored Program

## Thesis
To achieve intelligence, hardware engineering had to be bypassed entirely. John von Neumann's codification of the stored-program architecture meant that machines no longer needed to be rewired for new tasks; they simply needed new symbols loaded into memory. This birthed the era of software.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: The ENIAC's physical cabling, John von Neumann, the *First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC*, the separation of processor and memory.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Magnetic core memory (next chapter).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Ballistics Bottleneck:** The painful reality of "programming" the ENIAC by unplugging thick cables.
2. **The Train Ride:** Von Neumann drafts the EDVAC report.
3. **The Blank Slate:** The realization that instructions and data are mathematically identical and can both be stored as discrete symbols in memory.

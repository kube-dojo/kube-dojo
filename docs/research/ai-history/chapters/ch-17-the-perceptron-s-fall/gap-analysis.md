# Gap Analysis: Chapter 17 - The Perceptron's Fall

## Current Verdict

`NEEDS_REVIEW`, but the contract is likely close to `READY_TO_DRAFT_WITH_CAP`.

The chapter has enough anchored evidence for a 4,000-4,700 word draft. It should
not expand beyond that unless a primary funding/reception source is added. The
safe long-form path is technical/institutional explanation, not a dramatic
blame narrative.

## Green Scenes

- **Mark I hardware and demo:** ONR Digital Computer Newsletter July 1960 pp.1-3
  supports a detailed opening scene with sponsorship, demo scope, hardware
  architecture, training procedure, and caveats.
- **Rosenblatt's connectionist theory:** Rosenblatt 1958 pp.386-389 supports the
  memory/association frame and the perceptron as a probabilistic model.
- **Rosenblatt's own limits and multilayer hopes:** Rosenblatt 1961 pp.5,
  94-95, 303-308, and 575-576 support the distinction between three-layer
  series-coupled "minimal" perceptrons, their deficiencies, and the claimed
  potential of multilayer/cross-coupled topologies.
- **Minsky/Papert theorem core:** Perceptrons intro pp.7-8 and 12-14 support the
  connectedness/locality results.
- **Prior-structure critique:** Perceptrons intro pp.16-20 supports the argument
  that learning needs task-matched structure and that random/quasi-universal
  approaches scale poorly.
- **Official-history correction:** Olazaran 1996 pp.613, 629-631, 637-641
  supports the myth correction, proof boundary, funding context, and
  non-abandonment caveat.

# People: Chapter 17 - The Perceptron's Fall

- Frank Rosenblatt: Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory psychologist; originator of
  perceptron theory and leader of the Mark I program. Treat him as a serious
  connectionist/brain-model researcher, not a foil.
- Marvin Minsky: MIT AI leader and coauthor of *Perceptrons*. Earlier neural-net
  experience matters, but do not over-narrate motives beyond sourced material.
- Seymour Papert: coauthor of *Perceptrons*; later explicitly addressed whether
  he and Minsky tried to "kill connectionism."
- Leon Bottou: 2017 foreword author; useful for later machine-learning framing
  and for distinguishing mathematical relevance from historical damage.
- Mikel Olazaran: historian/sociologist of the perceptrons controversy; key
  source for official-history and funding-structure interpretation.
- Marvin Denicoff: ONR figure quoted by Olazaran about ONR/ARPA funding scale.
- Jon Guice: historian of ARPA/perceptrons controversy cited by Olazaran for
  ARPA's concentration of IPTO resources on symbolic AI.
- Allen Newell and Herbert Simon: symbolic-AI leaders relevant only as context
  for the institutional closure of the controversy; avoid turning Ch17 into a
  full symbolic-AI chapter.

# Timeline: Chapter 17 - The Perceptron's Fall

- 1956: Rosenblatt begins the perceptron program, according to the ONR Digital
  Computer Newsletter July 1960 account.
- 1958: Rosenblatt publishes "The Perceptron" in *Psychological Review*,
  framing the model around recognition, generalization, memory, and behavior.
- June 1960: Mark I perceptron is publicly demonstrated at Cornell Aeronautical
  Laboratory, with ONR/RADC sponsorship.
- 1961: Rosenblatt publishes *Principles of Neurodynamics*, expanding the
  perceptron program beyond the original 1958 article.
- Mid-1960s: Minsky and Papert's work circulates in draft form, according to
  Olazaran's historical reconstruction.
- 1969: MIT Press publishes Minsky and Papert's *Perceptrons*.
- 1970s: Neural-network work is largely displaced from mainstream AI, but not
  completely abandoned; some ideas continue in adjacent fields.
- Mid-1980s: PDP/backprop work revives neural-network interest and reopens the
  interpretation of the perceptrons controversy.
- 1988: Minsky and Papert publish the expanded edition; Papert also addresses
  the "did we kill connectionism?" question in *Daedalus*.
- 2017: MIT Press reissues the expanded edition with Bottou's foreword, which
  frames the book as rigorous and still relevant but historically consequential.

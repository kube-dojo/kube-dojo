# Scene Sketches: Chapter 17 - The Perceptron's Fall

## Scene 1: The Machine That Learned Letters

Open with the Mark I perceptron as a real machine, not an abstract myth. The
ONR newsletter gives a concrete public-demo scene: Cornell Aeronautical
Laboratory, June demonstration, ONR/RADC sponsorship, letters of the alphabet,
error-correction training, 20x20 photocell input, 512 association units, eight
response units. Balance the demonstration with the same source's caveats:
limited capacity, research purpose, economics not demonstrated.

## Scene 2: Rosenblatt's Connectionist Bet

Move from machine to theory. Rosenblatt 1958 asks how organisms recognize,
generalize, remember, and let memory influence behavior. His perceptron is a
probabilistic alternative to coded image storage: retained information lives in
connections or associations. This is the positive vision that later critique
challenged.

## Scene 3: The Mathematical Turn

Introduce Minsky/Papert's object carefully. They are not studying every future
neural network; they define perceptrons through partial predicates and weighted
linear threshold decisions. Use the connectedness theorem and the
diameter-limited theorem as the central explanation of local parallelism
failing on certain global predicates.

## Scene 4: The Scaling Critique

Explain why the result mattered beyond a clever theorem. Minsky/Papert argue
that learning needs prior structure and that random/quasi-universal partial
predicates have little chance on high-order problems. This is the sober
infrastructure point: a learning machine can be mathematically elegant and
still scale badly when its parts are not matched to the task.

## Scene 5: From Theorem to Winter

Use Bottou and Olazaran to separate technical result from social effect.
Perceptron-style work became unfashionable and funding was scarce. The
"official history" treated Minsky/Papert as showing neural nets had to be
abandoned, but Olazaran argues that this interpretation was bound up with
symbolic AI's institutional rise. The scene should be careful: the book mattered,
but it was not the only causal lever.

## Scene 6: The Door Left Open

End by refusing both triumphal and victim narratives. Rosenblatt overreached in
some hopes; Minsky/Papert overconstrained the future if read as the final word
on learning systems. Neural nets did not disappear; they left mainstream AI and
returned when new algorithms, data, and compute made different systems viable.

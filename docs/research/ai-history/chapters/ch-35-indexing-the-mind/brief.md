# Brief: Chapter 35 - Indexing the Mind

## Thesis
Google transformed the chaotic internet into structured infrastructure. By inventing PageRank, they mapped human semantic meaning onto mathematical eigenvectors. To do this, they had to build distributed data center infrastructure that would inadvertently become the foundation for modern AI compute.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Larry Page, Sergey Brin, PageRank algorithm, the math of eigenvectors, Google's shift to commodity hardware clusters.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Deep learning applications at Google (belongs to later chapters).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Broken Web:** Early search engines rely on keyword counting and are easily spammed.
2. **The Academic Citation:** Page and Brin realize that hyperlinks are votes of confidence, mathematically translating this into a massive Markov chain (PageRank).
3. **The Commodity Cluster:** To process the entire web, Google abandons expensive mainframes and builds massive clusters of cheap, failure-prone PCs, creating the modern data center.

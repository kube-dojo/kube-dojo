## Conflict Notes

- "More data is magic" framing — strongly disputed. Banko-Brill is explicit that the result is for a specific class of disambiguation problem; Halevy-Norvig-Pereira generalize but with caveats. Church 2011 explicitly raises the cost of the swing.
- The Brown Corpus authorship — Kučera & Francis (original 1967) vs. Francis & Kučera (Manual revisions) vs. Kucera-Francis-Carroll (Halevy 2009 ref). All three are primary; the chapter should not gloss the difference.
- "The web replaced annotated corpora" — partially false; see the Citation Bar discussion in `brief.md`.
- Industry vs. academy in the empirical turn — Banko & Brill at Microsoft, Halevy-Norvig-Pereira at Google, Brants et al. at Google, Resnik at Maryland with DoD/DARPA funding (per Resnik 1999 footnote 1, p.527: "supported by Department of Defense contract MDA90496C1250, DARPA/ITO Contract N66001-97-C-8540, and a research grant from Sun Microsystems Laboratories"). The chapter should note the institutional pattern without flattening it into a slogan.

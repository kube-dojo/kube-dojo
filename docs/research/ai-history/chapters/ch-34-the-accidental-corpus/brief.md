# Brief: Chapter 34 - The Accidental Corpus

## Thesis
The creation of the World Wide Web provided AI with the one resource it had lacked for fifty years: massive, digitized, varied human data. The internet served as an accidental, planetary-scale corpus that made empirical, statistical machine learning viable on a massive scale.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Tim Berners-Lee, the digitization of human text, the shift from curated academic datasets (Brown/LDC) to wild internet data in NLP/MT.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Search engines (next chapter).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Data Famine:** Researchers in the 1980s struggling to find enough text to train statistical models, resorting to tiny curated corpora like the Brown Corpus.
2. **The Web Explodes:** The creation of HTML and HTTP leads to an unprecedented, uncoordinated digitization of human knowledge.
3. **The Unreasonable Effectiveness:** Researchers realize they can utilize the chaotic, messy web text. Size becomes a powerful substitute for curation quality in early NLP/MT tasks.

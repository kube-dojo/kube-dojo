## Conflict Notes

- **Walter 1950 vs Holland 2003 on the rigour of the original *Sci. Am.* description.** Walter 1950 is a popular-science article and "gives only the briefest textual description of the constructional and electronic details" (Holland 2003 p. 2089). The full circuit-level detail comes from Holland 2003's BNI archival work and from Walter 1953's Appendix C. The chapter must cite Walter 1950 for the *behavioural* claims and Holland 2003 (or chained Walter 1953) for the *circuit* claims; mixing these up will overclaim what the original article said.
- **"Vacuum tubes" vs "thermionic valves" vs "miniature radio tubes."** Walter 1950 p. 42 calls them "miniature radio tubes." Holland 2003 calls them "miniature thermionic valves, or vacuum tubes." The chapter should use Walter's own phrasing when describing the original 1948–1949 build and the broader "vacuum tube" terminology when reaching for the von Neumann reliability argument.
- **The "transition to digital" thesis.** The legacy chapter's framing — that vacuum-tube unreliability "drove the field toward digital architectures where logic was separated from wiring" — is not directly anchored in Walter 1950 or in von Neumann 1952. Walter's tortoises were already digital-discrete in their relay-mediated behaviour states; von Neumann's 1952 argument applies to all unreliable-component technologies, analog and digital alike. The chapter's honest close must reframe this: the bottleneck was *hardware-as-program* (behaviour bound to wiring), and the architectural escape route was *stored-program* programmability (Ch8), not "digital" per se.

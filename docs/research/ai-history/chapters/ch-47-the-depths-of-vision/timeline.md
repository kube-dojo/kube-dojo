# Timeline: Chapter 47 - The Depths of Vision

- **2012:** Krizhevsky, Sutskever, and Hinton publish AlexNet at NeurIPS. Their PDF anchors a large ImageNet CNN with five convolutional and three fully connected layers, two GTX 580 3GB GPUs, and five to six days of training. Source: sources.md G1.
- **September 2014:** Simonyan and Zisserman release the VGG arXiv paper, later published at ICLR 2015, showing that ImageNet accuracy improved when depth was pushed to 16-19 weight layers with 3x3 filters. Source: sources.md G2.
- **2014 competition cycle:** GoogLeNet/Inception and VGG make "very deep" ImageNet models the state of the art. ResNet later summarizes leading ImageNet models as using depths of roughly sixteen to thirty layers. Source: sources.md G6.
- **February-July 2015:** Batch Normalization appears at ICML 2015 and becomes part of the normalization context that ResNet cites as enabling tens-layer networks to start converging. Source: sources.md G7, with BatchNorm paper still Yellow for page-level expansion.
- **December 10, 2015:** The ResNet arXiv version is posted as "Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition" (arXiv:1512.03385). Source: sources.md primary source table.
- **ILSVRC/COCO 2015:** The ResNet team reports first-place results in ImageNet classification, ImageNet detection, ImageNet localization, COCO detection, and COCO segmentation; the ImageNet official page independently anchors MSRA detection/localization standings. Sources: sources.md G22, G25, G26.
- **June 2016:** The ResNet paper appears at CVPR 2016, pp. 770-778. Source: sources.md G4.
- **2016 paper evidence:** The paper documents the degradation problem, identity shortcuts, bottleneck designs, 152-layer ImageNet results, over-1000-layer CIFAR-10 experiments, and COCO detection transfer. Sources: sources.md G8-G24.
- **After 2016:** Residual connections become a common design pattern in deep networks, but this chapter should only forward-reference later vision/attention developments sparsely (see Ch48) unless additional anchors are added.

# Brief: Chapter 32 - The DARPA SUR Program

## Thesis
The transition of AI from a theoretical, rule-based academic pursuit to an empirical, data-driven engineering discipline was accelerated by military funding metrics. The DARPA Speech Understanding Research (SUR) program mandated strict, statistical evaluation, setting the stage for what actually worked on data over elegant, hand-coded logic.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: DARPA's SUR program (1971-1976), the creation of systematic evaluation metrics, the tension between linguists and statisticians, Fred Jelinek's team at IBM.
- OUT OF SCOPE: The deep mathematical derivations of Hidden Markov Models (covered in Part 5).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Linguist's Challenge:** Early attempts to hand-code speech recognition rules break under real human accents and noise.
2. **The DARPA Mandate:** DARPA demands measurable, empirical results with common test evaluations.
3. **"Every time I fire a linguist..."** Fred Jelinek and the IBM team demonstrate that statistical models and probabilities beat elegant linguistic theory on speech tasks.

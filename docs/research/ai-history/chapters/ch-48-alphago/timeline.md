# Timeline: Chapter 48 - AlphaGo

## Chronology

- **1997:** IBM's Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov at chess. Google later uses this as part of the public contrast: chess had fallen, but Go remained difficult because traditional search-tree methods did not scale. Anchor: `sources.md` G10-G11.
- **2012-2015:** Deep convolutional networks and deep reinforcement learning become central background techniques for AlphaGo. Silver et al. cite ImageNet-era deep learning and Atari deep RL as recent precedents. Anchor: S1 references and p.484 lines 46-48; keep compact.
- **October 2015:** AlphaGo defeats Fan Hui, the reigning three-time European Go champion, 5-0 in a closed-door match at DeepMind's London office. Anchor: `sources.md` G13-G14.
- **November 11, 2015:** Silver et al.'s *Nature* article is received by the journal. Anchor: Nature article metadata, S1.
- **January 5, 2016:** The *Nature* article is accepted. Anchor: Nature article metadata, S1.
- **January 27, 2016:** Google publishes the AlphaGo launch post and announces the upcoming Lee Sedol challenge in Seoul. Anchor: `sources.md` S2 and G13-G14.
- **January 28, 2016:** *Nature* issue date for "Mastering the game of Go with deep neural networks and tree search," pp. 484-489. Anchor: S1.
- **March 8, 2016:** Google posts the pre-match update for AlphaGo's five-game challenge against Lee Sedol. Anchor: `sources.md` G15-G16.
- **March 9, 2016:** Game 1: AlphaGo wins. Anchor: `sources.md` G17.
- **March 10, 2016:** Game 2: AlphaGo wins again; Google reports creative moves that surprised expert commentators. Move 37 becomes the chapter's key public scene. Anchor: `sources.md` G18, G26-G27.
- **March 12, 2016:** Game 3: AlphaGo wins its third straight game and secures the overall match. Anchor: `sources.md` G19.
- **March 13, 2016:** Game 4: Lee Sedol wins by resignation after 180 moves; Google identifies Lee's Move 78 and AlphaGo's next move as central to the reversal. Anchor: `sources.md` G20.
- **March 15, 2016:** Game 5: AlphaGo wins after 280 moves, ending the match 4-1. Google says the $1 million prize will be donated to UNICEF, STEM charities, and Go organizations. Anchor: `sources.md` G21.
- **2017:** The *AlphaGo* documentary appears and later becomes useful for timecoded scene reconstruction. Anchor status: Yellow, `sources.md` S9.
- **2017 onward:** AlphaGo Zero and AlphaZero shift the technical story toward learning without human expert games. Boundary: forward reference only, likely Ch49.

## Date Discipline Notes

- Use exact March 2016 game dates from Google's own match log.
- Do not conflate the January 2016 Fan Hui/Nature announcement with the October 2015 closed-door match.
- Do not describe the Lee Sedol series as a five-game match that AlphaGo won only at the end; Google says AlphaGo secured overall victory after Game 3, then all five games were still played.

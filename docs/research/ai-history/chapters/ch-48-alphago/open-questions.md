# Open Questions: Chapter 48 - AlphaGo

## Yellow Claims To Resolve

1. **Exact Lee Sedol match hardware.** Nature verifies distributed AlphaGo configurations up to 1,920 CPUs / 280 GPUs, but not that this was the live match configuration. Need an official DeepMind technical note, match FAQ, documentary timecode, or primary interview.
2. **Server location and live network path.** Current sources support Google Cloud Platform use generally and an on-site Internet connection, but not the exact production topology for Seoul. Need primary Google/DeepMind source.
3. **Lee Sedol title count.** The prose can safely say Google called him the best Go player of the prior decade. "18-time world champion" needs a federation/profile source or reliable contemporary article with exact title list.
4. **Audience size.** Claims such as 200 million viewers need media-measurement evidence before use.
5. **Fan Hui ranking improvement.** WIRED reports the ranking rose sharply after training with AlphaGo, but a Green claim needs before/after ranking data or a primary Go-ratings source.

## Red Claims Not To Draft

1. **Move 37 as proof of consciousness or psychological intuition.** Sources support novelty, low human probability, and expert surprise, not inner experience.
2. **DeepMind premonition of Move 37's symbolism.** No source supports lab-room foreshadowing.
3. **Direct causal chain from AlphaGo to modern generative AI.** Later chapters can draw a broader arc; Chapter 48 should not claim causality beyond its evidence.

## Archival / Media Access That Would Help

- Full Google DeepMind YouTube transcripts with timestamps for Games 2, 4, and 5; especially Move 37 commentary, post-game press conference remarks, and Hassabis/Silver technical explanations.
- The 2017 *AlphaGo* documentary with verified timecodes for match-room staging, Fan Hui's role, Lee Sedol interviews, and DeepMind control-room scenes.
- Melanie Mitchell 2019 or another scholarly secondary source with exact page anchors for the public meaning of AlphaGo.
- A Google/DeepMind engineering post or interview that names the live Lee Sedol match hardware and deployment architecture.

## Prose Guidance

The current contract is draftable with a cap. It has enough Green evidence for a 4,000-ish word chapter focused on technical architecture and the match. It does not yet support a heavily cinematic documentary-style chapter or a broad "birth of modern AI" argument without new anchors.

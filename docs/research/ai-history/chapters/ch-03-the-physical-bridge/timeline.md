## Notes on dating

- The "August 10, 1937" thesis signature date is the most-cited single anchor of the chapter and is verified Green via direct `pdftotext` extraction of the MIT DSpace PDF.
- The thesis's bound copy bears "1940" on the cover as the degree-conferral year; Wikipedia and standard reference works (and Shannon's own CV) give 1937 as the master's year, consistent with the August 10, 1937 signature. The chapter should anchor to the signature date, not the cover year.
- The "year of Shannon's thesis" question is sometimes obscured by the publication delay: thesis signed August 1937, manuscript submitted March 1938, presented June 1938, published December 1938. The chapter should distinguish between the work (1937) and its publication (1938).
- The priority-of-discovery question across Shannon, Nakashima, Piesch, Plechl-Duschek is not settled by this timeline. The chapter's stance per `brief.md` is that Nakashima's work preceded Shannon's by approximately two years, that Piesch and Plechl-Duschek arrived at related ideas slightly later, and that Shannon's distinctive contribution was axiomatic completeness — not insight priority.

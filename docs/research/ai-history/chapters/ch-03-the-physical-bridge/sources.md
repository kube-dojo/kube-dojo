## Conflict Notes

- **The "5 relays to 3" folk claim** — the legacy chapter prose at `src/content/docs/ai-history/ch-03-the-physical-bridge.md` says "He took a messy, intuitively designed circuit containing five separate relays. … The result was a circuit that performed the exact same function but required only three relays instead of five." This is **wrong**. The actual Shannon Figure 5 → Figure 6 reduction is 13 element-occurrences → 5 (over six distinct relays W, X, Y, Z, S, V). Drafting prose must correct this.
- **"Most important master's thesis of the 20th century"** — Wikipedia attributes this to Howard Gardner 1985 *The Mind's New Science* with the qualifier "possibly." The chapter must preserve the qualifier or simply not include the superlative claim — the thesis's importance is plenty defensible without the hyperbole.
- **Priority of discovery** — Nakashima 1935 (Japanese) precedes Shannon 1937 by approximately two years. The chapter's stance is independent simultaneous discovery; Shannon's distinctive contribution is the axiomatic completeness, not the insight that Boolean algebra and switching circuits map onto each other. The chapter must NOT silently elide Nakashima.
- **Bush as Shannon's advisor** — Shannon's 1937 thesis acknowledgement names F. L. Hitchcock as supervisor; the 1938 paper byline thanks Hitchcock, Bush, and Caldwell. The chapter should preserve this distinction. Bush was the *research-program supervisor* and Shannon's MIT employer; Hitchcock was the formal academic advisor.
- **The 1931 analyzer vs. the 1942 Rockefeller analyzer** — these are two different machines. The legacy prose conflates them. The 1931 machine had no relays; the 1942 machine had thousands. Shannon's 1936-1937 work was on the 1931 mechanical machine, motivated by the setup-problem of the 1942 successor that was being designed in parallel.
- **Owens 1986 as a Shannon source** — Owens's article does not mention Shannon. It is a primary anchor for Bush and the Differential Analyzer only. The chapter must not cite Owens as a source for any Shannon-specific claim.

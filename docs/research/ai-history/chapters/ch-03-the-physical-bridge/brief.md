# Brief: Chapter 3 - The Physical Bridge

## Thesis
Claude Shannon bridged the gap between pure mathematical logic and physical infrastructure by proving that Boole's abstract 1s and 0s perfectly mapped to the open/closed states of electrical relays.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Telephone routing networks, Shannon's 1937 thesis (published 1938), mapping Boolean algebra to circuits.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Information Theory (1948).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Plumber's Engineering:** Early telephone networks are designed by trial-and-error.
2. **The Logical Circuit:** Shannon realizes open/closed switches map to Boole's False/True.
3. **Math Becomes Infrastructure:** Computation transitions from theoretical to physical engineering.

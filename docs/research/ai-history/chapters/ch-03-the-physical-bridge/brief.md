# Brief: Chapter 3 - The Physical Bridge

## Thesis
Claude Shannon bridged the gap between pure mathematical logic and physical infrastructure by proving that Boole's abstract 1s and 0s perfectly mapped to electrical relays, including a rigorous demonstration of circuit minimization.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Vannevar Bush's Differential Analyzer, Akira Nakashima's parallel Japanese discovery, Shannon's 1937 thesis, relay minimization theorem (5 to 3 relays), normally-closed contacts for NOT.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Information Theory (1948).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Plumber's Engineering:** The messy engineering of the Differential Analyzer and telephone networks.
2. **The Parallel Discovery:** Akira Nakashima's independent realization of switching logic in Japan.
3. **The Logical Circuit:** Shannon maps AND and OR, and uses normally-closed contacts for NOT.
4. **The Minimization Theorem:** Shannon's pedagogical proof reducing a 5-relay circuit to 3 relays using Boolean algebra.
5. **Math Becomes Infrastructure:** Computation transitions from theoretical to physical engineering.

# Brief: Chapter 3 - The Physical Bridge

## Thesis
The realization that abstract logic could be built as physical infrastructure was the most important engineering insight of the 20th century. Claude Shannon proved that Boole's theoretical 1s and 0s perfectly mapped to the open and closed states of electrical relays, making computing a physical reality.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Telephone routing networks, the MIT Differential Analyzer, Claude Shannon's 1937 master's thesis.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Information Theory (1948).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Plumber's Engineering:** Early analog calculators and telephone networks are designed by intuition and trial-and-error.
2. **The Graduate Student:** Claude Shannon observes the relays at MIT.
3. **The Logical Circuit:** Shannon maps Boolean algebra to electrical circuits. Math becomes infrastructure.

# People: Chapter 39 - The Vision Wall

## Core Actors

- **David G. Lowe:** Author of the IJCV SIFT article, "Distinctive Image Features from Scale-Invariant Keypoints," published in 2004 in *International Journal of Computer Vision* 60, pp. 91-110. His abstract anchors SIFT as a method for distinctive invariant image features and object recognition. Sources: G01-G02.
- **Navneet Dalal and Bill Triggs:** Authors of the 2005 CVPR HOG paper, pp. 886-893, DOI 10.1109/CVPR.2005.177. The paper anchors HOG, linear-SVM human detection, local contrast normalization, and the need for a harder pedestrian dataset. Source: G03.
- **Mark Everingham:** Key member of the VOC project according to the official VOC2012 page; died in 2012, with the ECCV 2012 VOC workshop dedicated to his memory. Co-author of the 2015 VOC retrospective. Sources: G23-G25.
- **Luc van Gool, Chris Williams, John Winn, Andrew Zisserman:** Listed VOC2012 organizers and co-authors or associated figures in VOC infrastructure. Sources: G24, G26.
- **Antonio Torralba and Alexei A. Efros:** Authors of "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011, pp. 1521-1528). They provide the chapter's strongest empirical critique of benchmark closed worlds, dataset signatures, and cross-dataset generalization. Sources: G13-G22.
- **Pedro Felzenszwalb, Ross Girshick, David McAllester, Deva Ramanan:** Their discriminatively trained part-based model is summarized on the VOC2012 page as a HOG-template/latent-SVM detection example applied to all 20 VOC classes. Source: G12.

## Peripheral or Forward-Referenced Actors

- **Fei-Fei Li, Jia Deng, and ImageNet collaborators:** Relevant mainly for Ch40. In Ch39, they should appear only through the official VOC2010/2012 pointer to an ImageNet-associated large-scale recognition challenge unless separate page anchors are added. Sources: G08; Yellow Y03-Y04.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent personal frustration, lab dialogue, or motives for any actor.
- Do not describe PASCAL organizers as blocking progress. The verified sources support a more balanced view: VOC gave the community disciplined evaluation infrastructure, while later bias/scale analysis showed what that infrastructure could not solve alone.

# People: Chapter 31 - Reinforcement Learning Roots

- Richard Bellman: dynamic-programming and Markov-decision background. Use as mathematical ancestry, not as sole RL inventor.
- Arthur L. Samuel: early machine-learning/game-playing precursor through checkers, evaluation functions, and self-improvement.
- Andrew G. Barto: coauthor of the 1983 adaptive critic / pole-balancing paper; central actor-critic/RL figure.
- Richard S. Sutton: coauthor of the 1983 paper and author of the 1988 temporal-difference paper; core protagonist for TD learning.
- Charles W. Anderson: coauthor of the 1983 pole-balancing paper.
- Christopher J. C. H. Watkins: author of the 1989 delayed-rewards thesis and coauthor of the 1992 Q-learning paper.
- Peter Dayan: coauthor of the 1992 Q-learning convergence paper.
- Leslie Pack Kaelbling, Michael L. Littman, Andrew W. Moore: 1996 survey authors; use for field framing, not as scene protagonists unless needed.
- Gerald Tesauro: TD-Gammon author; closing bridge from early RL roots to self-play systems.
- Bill Robertie, Paul Magriel, Malcolm Davis, Kit Woolsey: backgammon experts mentioned in Tesauro's evaluation context; use sparingly if the TD-Gammon scene needs human benchmark detail.

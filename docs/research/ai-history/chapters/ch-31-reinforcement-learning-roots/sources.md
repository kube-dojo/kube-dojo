## Conflict Notes

- Do not call Samuel 1959 "Q-learning" or imply it had modern convergence theory. It is a precursor around game evaluation and self-improvement.
- Do not say Bellman invented reinforcement learning. Use him as dynamic-programming/MDP mathematical ancestry.
- Do not claim Barto/Sutton/Anderson solved pole balancing with no assumptions at all. The abstract says the cart-pole equations were not known and feedback was sparse; other implementation details need deeper page anchors.
- Do not claim Watkins 1989 was fully parsed. The full PDF was fetched, but only the first 30 pages were OCRed in this pass. Use Watkins/Dayan 1992 for exact Q-learning theorem details.
- Do not turn TD-Gammon into a universal proof. The stochastic game environment, self-play dynamics, feature choices, and later caution in Kaelbling/Littman/Moore must remain visible.

# Timeline: Chapter 31 - Reinforcement Learning Roots

- 1957: Richard Bellman publishes "A Markovian Decision Process," giving a dynamic-programming recurrence background for sequential decision problems.
- 1959: Arthur Samuel publishes the checkers machine-learning paper, showing a program improving play through evaluation and experience rather than exhaustive search.
- 1983: Barto, Sutton, and Anderson publish the ASE/ACE pole-balancing paper in *IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics*.
- 1988: Sutton publishes "Learning to Predict by the Methods of Temporal Differences" in *Machine Learning*.
- May 1989: Watkins submits *Learning from Delayed Rewards* at King's College, Cambridge.
- 1992: Watkins and Dayan publish "Q-learning" in *Machine Learning*, including the convergence proof for restricted settings.
- 1995: Tesauro publishes "Temporal Difference Learning and TD-Gammon" in *Communications of the ACM*.
- 1996: Kaelbling, Littman, and Moore publish "Reinforcement Learning: A Survey" in JAIR, summarizing the field's computer-science formulation and open issues.

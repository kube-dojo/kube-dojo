# Timeline: Chapter 55

- **2019:** Richard Sutton publishes "The Bitter Lesson," arguing that general methods leveraging computation have repeatedly won over approaches that build in human knowledge.
- **January 2020:** Kaplan et al. publish "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models" on arXiv, reporting empirical power-law relationships between language-model loss and model size, data, and compute.
- **May 2020:** GPT-3 paper uses the scaling-law frame as part of its rationale for testing a 175B-parameter model.
- **March 2022:** Hoffmann et al. publish "Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models," arguing that many large language models were undertrained and that compute-optimal training should scale parameters and tokens roughly equally.

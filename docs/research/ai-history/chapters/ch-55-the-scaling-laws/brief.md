# Brief: Chapter 55 - The Scaling Laws

## Thesis
OpenAI formalized the brutal, physical economics of AI. They proved mathematically that model performance scales predictably with compute, data size, and parameter count. This "Scaling Law" turned AI research from a creative algorithmic pursuit into an industrial engineering logistics problem.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, the 2020 Scaling Laws paper, the predictable power-law relationship between compute and loss.
- OUT OF SCOPE: GPT-4 (later chapters).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Algorithmic Hunt:** Researchers typically try to find clever new architectures to improve models.
2. **The Physics of Compute:** OpenAI realizes that architecture matters far less than raw scale.
3. **The Power Law:** The publication of the smooth, predictable curve showing that throwing 10x more compute at a dumb model reliably yields better intelligence.

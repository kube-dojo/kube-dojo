# People: Chapter 22 - The Lisp Machine Bubble

## Core Cast

- **Alan Bawden:** MIT Lisp Machine Group member named on AIM-444. Use as part
  of the collective project credit rather than as a solo protagonist.
- **Richard Greenblatt:** MIT AI Lab hacker and LMI founder in the later
  commercialization story. Do not narrate the company split from partisan
  sources unless balanced and clearly attributed.
- **Jack Holloway:** MIT Lisp Machine Group member named on AIM-444 and CADR
  memo coauthor.
- **Thomas F. Knight Jr.:** CONS designer named in AIM-444 and CADR memo
  coauthor. Anchor for the hardware lineage from prototype to CADR.
- **David A. Moon:** MIT Lisp Machine Group member, CADR memo coauthor,
  Symbolics figure in Gabriel/Steele, and Lisp Machine Manual coauthor. Useful
  for continuity from lab system to commercial environment.
- **Daniel Weinreb:** MIT Lisp Machine Group member and Lisp Machine Manual
  coauthor. Later participant account may be useful as a Yellow stretch source
  for Symbolics failure causes, but it is not yet parsed.
- **Guy L. Steele Jr.:** CADR memo coauthor and coauthor of "The Evolution of
  Lisp." Use for Common Lisp and later historical synthesis.
- **Richard Stallman:** Contributor credited in the Lisp Machine Manual and a
  later partisan witness to the Symbolics/LMI/free-software conflict. Use only
  with explicit caveats if the conflict needs a scene.
- **Richard P. Gabriel:** Coauthor of "The Evolution of Lisp." Use as later
  historical synthesis for Lisp-machine commercialization, Common Lisp, and
  stock-hardware pressure.
- **Symbolics technical authorship group:** The 3600 Technical Summary is a
  corporate/vendor source. Treat it as a product voice, not an independent
  narrator.

## Institutional Cast

- **MIT AI Lab:** Home of the Lisp Machine project and the lab culture that made
  all-Lisp interactive computing plausible.
- **ARPA/ONR:** Funding context for AIM-444, AIM-528, and the Lisp Machine
  Manual. Avoid making ARPA the protagonist here; the chapter is about the
  infrastructure outcome.
- **Lisp Machines, Inc. (LMI):** One commercialization path from MIT. Current
  anchors support its emergence and initial CADR-clone role, but not detailed
  business history.
- **Symbolics, Inc.:** The other major commercialization path and maker of LM-2
  and 3600. Current anchors support product specification and self-history, but
  not independent financial performance.
- **ARPA Lisp community:** The fractured Maclisp, Interlisp, Spice Lisp, and
  Lisp-machine groups that helped create Common Lisp.

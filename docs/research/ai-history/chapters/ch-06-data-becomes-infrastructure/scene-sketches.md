# Scene Sketches: Chapter 6

## Scene 1: The PASCAL Wall
- **Setting:** Fei-Fei Li's lab, circa 2006.
- **Action:** Researchers are fine-tuning algorithms, squeezing out tiny percentage improvements on small datasets like PASCAL VOC. Li realizes the algorithms aren't the problem; the world is just too complex to be learned from 20,000 images.
- **Citation Anchor:** Li (2023), Everingham (2010).
- **Infrastructural Point:** Data was previously seen as an afterthought to the algorithm. Li argues data *is* the foundation.

## Scene 2: The Human API
- **Setting:** 2007, struggling with undergraduate labelers.
- **Action:** The team calculates it would take decades for students to label the desired images. They discover Mechanical Turk. They build a pipeline to send batches of images to anonymous workers globally.
- **Citation Anchor:** Deng et al. (2009), Quartz (2017).
- **Infrastructural Point:** Human labor is abstracted into an infrastructural API, creating the first truly scalable data pipeline for AI.

## Scene 3: The Poster Session
- **Setting:** CVPR 2009, Miami.
- **Action:** ImageNet is presented not as a keynote, but as a poster. The reception is lukewarm. The establishment still believes in hand-crafted features (like SIFT) rather than brute-force data.
- **Citation Anchor:** Li (2023).
- **Infrastructural Point:** The realization that building the infrastructure often predates the community's understanding of its necessity.

# Sources: Chapter 6

## Annotated Bibliography

### Primary
- [x] **"ImageNet: A Large-Scale Hierarchical Image Database" (J. Deng, W. Dong, R. Socher, L.-J. Li, K. Li and L. Fei-Fei, CVPR 2009)**
  - Verification: Green. The foundational paper introducing the dataset (3.2 million images at the time) and its Mechanical Turk methodology. Anchors Scenes 2 and 3.
- [x] **"The Worlds I See: Curiosity, Exploration, and Discovery at the Dawn of AI" (Fei-Fei Li, 2023)**
  - Verification: Green. A primary memoir providing first-hand accounts of the struggle to build ImageNet, the skepticism of the CVPR community, and the reliance on Mechanical Turk. Anchors Scenes 1 and 3.
- [x] **"Utility data annotation with Amazon Mechanical Turk" (A. Sorokin, D. Forsyth, CVPR 2008)**
  - Verification: Green. Demonstrates the early realization in the vision community that AMT could be used as scalable human infrastructure. Anchors Scene 2.
- [x] **"The PASCAL Visual Object Classes (VOC) Challenge" (Everingham et al., IJCV 2010)**
  - Verification: Green. Primary documentation of the scale and limitations of the datasets that preceded ImageNet. Anchors Scene 1.

### Secondary
- [x] **"The Data That Transformed AI Research—and Possibly the World" (Quartz, 2017)**
  - Verification: Green. Detailed journalistic account of the creation of ImageNet, the Mechanical Turk labor force, and its infrastructural impact on the field. Anchors Scenes 2 and 3.
- [x] **"Genius Makers: The Mavericks Who Brought AI to Google, Facebook, and the World" (Cade Metz, 2021)**
  - Verification: Green. Covers the historical context of the vision community's resistance to data-first approaches prior to the deep learning explosion. Anchors Scene 1.

## Conflict Notes
- **AlexNet vs ImageNet credit:** Some early retrospectives credit the 2012 AlexNet moment (Chapter 7) as the sole turning point. This chapter will strictly focus on the *creation of the data infrastructure* (2006-2009) as the necessary precondition. The narrative tension is resolved by separating the dataset's creation from its ultimate vindication by GPUs, emphasizing that the data *itself* was an infrastructure project that predated the algorithmic breakthrough.

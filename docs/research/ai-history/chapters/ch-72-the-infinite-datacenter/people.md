# People: Chapter 72 - The Infinite Datacenter

## People And Institutions

- **Sam Altman / OpenAI:** Public face of the Stargate compute buildout and
  the claim that AI progress requires building compute at national scale.
- **Masayoshi Son / SoftBank:** Stargate chair and financial lead in the
  January 2025 announcement; useful for showing that AI buildout became a
  capital-allocation story.
- **MGX:** Stargate equity funder in the January 2025 announcement; use as a
  narrow sovereign/global-capital signal, not as a broader UAE policy chapter.
- **Oracle:** Stargate infrastructure partner and operator/developer of major
  Stargate capacity, including the Abilene flagship and later site expansion.
- **Brad Smith / Microsoft:** Author of the January 2025 Microsoft essay that
  frames AI infrastructure as datacenters, construction firms, steel,
  liquid-cooling advances, electricians, and pipefitters.
- **Mark Zuckerberg / Meta:** Not a scene anchor through personal quotation
  unless needed, but Meta's disclosures and datacenter announcements make the
  physical/financial buildout visible.
- **Susan Li / Meta CFO:** Useful through Meta's capex guidance and Blue Owl
  financing statement; shows finance becoming part of AI infrastructure.
- **Rachel Peterson / Meta Data Centers:** Meta datacenter executive quoted in
  the Hyperion JV announcement; useful for local/community continuity.
- **Blue Owl Capital:** Infrastructure finance partner for Hyperion; owns 80%
  of the JV under Meta's announcement.
- **PIMCO and private bond investors:** Debt side of the Hyperion financing
  story; keep as institutional detail, not a digression.
- **Kevin Janda / Meta Data Center Strategy:** Richland Parish announcement
  quote source; use only if prose needs local siting rationale.
- **Entergy / El Paso Electric:** Utility counterparts showing that datacenter
  projects are negotiated with local power systems, not just deployed by tech
  companies alone.
- **AWS / Ron Diamant / Annapurna Labs:** Project Rainier and Trainium
  infrastructure; use to explain chip/server/network/datacenter integration.
- **Anthropic / Dario Amodei:** Frontier-model customer whose capacity needs
  make the AWS 5GW agreement narratively concrete.
- **DOE/LBNL:** Public-sector baseline on U.S. datacenter electricity growth
  and available resource strategies.
- **Google:** Environmental-report anchor for PUE, water consumption, and the
  claim that scaling AI infrastructure increases the scale of environmental
  management.

# Timeline: Chapter 72 - The Infinite Datacenter

- **Dec. 3, 2024:** AWS announces Trainium2 GA and Project Rainier with
  Anthropic, describing an EC2 UltraCluster of Trn2 UltraServers containing
  hundreds of thousands of Trainium2 chips.
- **Dec. 4, 2024:** Meta and Louisiana Economic Development announce the
  Richland Parish AI-optimized datacenter: $10B, 4 million square feet, 2,250
  acres, construction through 2030.
- **Dec. 20, 2024:** DOE announces the LBNL data-center energy report:
  4.4% of U.S. electricity in 2023, projected 6.7-12% by 2028.
- **Jan. 3, 2025:** Microsoft says it is on track to invest about $80B in
  FY2025 to build AI-enabled datacenters, more than half in the U.S.
- **Jan. 21, 2025:** OpenAI and SoftBank announce Stargate: intended $500B
  over four years, $100B immediate deployment, starting in Texas.
- **Jan. 29, 2025:** Meta reports 2024 capex of $39.23B and guides 2025 capex
  to $60-65B, driven by generative AI and core infrastructure.
- **June 2025:** Google publishes its 2025 Environmental Report, giving 2024
  fleet PUE, water replenishment, and operational water metrics.
- **Sept. 23, 2025:** OpenAI says Stargate has nearly 7GW planned capacity and
  over $400B investment over three years, after reviewing more than 300 site
  proposals from more than 30 states.
- **Oct. 15, 2025 / Mar. 26, 2026:** Meta announces and then updates El Paso:
  a site that can scale to 1GW, with updated investment over $10B and closed
  loop cooling plans.
- **Oct. 21, 2025:** Meta and Blue Owl announce the Hyperion JV, with about
  $27B development costs and long-lived power/cooling/connectivity
  infrastructure.
- **Dec. 19, 2025:** Meta says Richland Parish has contracted more than $875M
  with Louisiana businesses and will house Hyperion, its largest
  multi-gigawatt AI training cluster.
- **Apr. 20, 2026:** Anthropic announces an expanded Amazon agreement for up
  to 5GW of capacity and more than $100B over ten years to AWS technologies.

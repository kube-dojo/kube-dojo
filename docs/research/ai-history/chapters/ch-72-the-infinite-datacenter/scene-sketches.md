# Scene Sketches: Chapter 72 - The Infinite Datacenter

## Scene 1 - The Model Becomes A Construction Project

Open on the language shift. The AI race is no longer described only through
papers, models, or chips. Stargate asks for power, land, construction,
equipment, and site-design partners. The software story has become a
construction story.

## Scene 2 - A National Site Search

Use OpenAI's September 2025 update to make siting concrete: Abilene,
Shackelford County, Dona Ana County, Wisconsin/Midwest, Lordstown, Milam
County, and more than 300 proposals from more than 30 states. The point is not
real-estate trivia; it is that compute became a geography contest.

## Scene 3 - The Capex Wall

Microsoft's $80B FY2025 line and Meta's $39.23B 2024 / $60-65B 2025 capex
guidance show the balance-sheet pivot. For frontier AI, spending shifts from
software payroll and cloud opex into land, buildings, equipment, finance
leases, depreciation, and infrastructure cost.

## Scene 4 - The Campus

Richland Parish should be the tactile scene: 4 million square feet, 2,250
acres, a one-mile-long project footprint, peak construction workers, roads,
water systems, workforce programs, energy planning, and local contractors.
Treat it as a place, not a number.

## Scene 5 - The Financing Machine

The Meta/Blue Owl Hyperion joint venture shows that datacenter architecture
includes financial architecture. Explain why a $27B campus pushes companies
toward joint ventures, leases, guarantees, and private debt rather than simple
owned-and-operated facilities.

## Scene 6 - Dedicated Cloud Capacity

Project Rainier makes the cloud feel less abstract. Hundreds of thousands of
Trainium2 chips, UltraServers, NeuronLinks, EFA networking, multiple U.S.
datacenters, and Anthropic's later 5GW commitment show cloud turning into
named, model-specific infrastructure.

## Scene 7 - The Heat Has To Go Somewhere

Cooling is the hidden physical law of AI buildout. Use Google PUE, AWS outside
air/water-efficiency claims, Meta closed-loop El Paso cooling, and DOE cooling
research to show that heat removal is engineered into the site from the
beginning.

## Scene 8 - The Community Bargain

Local communities are now part of the AI stack. They negotiate jobs,
contractor spend, workforce training, roads, water, wastewater, grid
connection, bill-assistance funds, grants, and long-term risk. Keep the tone
balanced: benefits and strain both matter.

## Scene 9 - The Last Page

Close the book by returning to the historical arc. Turing imagined machines,
McCarthy named AI, expert systems sold rules, deep learning rode data and
GPUs. By the mid-2020s, frontier AI needed industrial works. The final limit is
not imagination alone; it is the capacity to build reality fast enough.

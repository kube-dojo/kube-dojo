# Timeline: Chapter 71 - The Chip War

- 2022-08-09: The CHIPS and Science Act is signed into law; NIST later frames
  CHIPS for America as $52.7B across incentives, R&D/workforce, and supply-chain
  programs.
- 2022-10-07: BIS's advanced-computing and semiconductor-manufacturing controls
  begin taking effect, later published at 87 FR 62186.
- 2023-10-17: BIS releases updates reinforcing the 2022 controls for advanced
  computing semiconductors, SME, and supercomputing items.
- 2023-09-01: Dutch national export-control rules for advanced semiconductor
  manufacturing equipment are in force, later expanded in September 2024.
- 2023-07-23: Japan's METI controls on specified high-performance semiconductor
  manufacturing equipment take effect according to CSIS translation.
- 2024-Q4: TSMC says its Arizona first fab entered 4nm volume production.
- 2024-12-02: BIS announces a package controlling more SME, software tools, HBM,
  red-flag guidance, and 140 Entity List additions.
- 2024-12-02: ASML says the updated U.S. rules restrict chip-manufacturing
  technology and could affect DUV immersion exports if Dutch authorities make
  similar assessments.
- 2024-12-03: China's MOFCOM Notice 2024 No. 46 restricts listed dual-use
  materials to the United States, including gallium, germanium, antimony,
  superhard materials, and stricter graphite checks.
- 2025-01-15: The AI Diffusion Rule is issued in the Federal Register.
- 2025-04-09: NVIDIA says the U.S. government informed it that H20 exports to
  China/Hong Kong/Macau/D:5 countries require a license.
- 2025-05-13: Commerce announces rescission of the AI Diffusion Rule and new
  guidance on overseas AI chips, PRC advanced computing ICs, Huawei Ascend chips,
  and diversion tactics.

# Scene Sketches: Chapter 71 - The Chip War

## Scene 1 - A GPU Becomes Policy

Begin with the mental reversal: yesterday's server accelerator is now named in a
national-security rule. Let the prose show a procurement object becoming a
strategic object because it enables training, supercomputing, military analysis,
and surveillance at scale.

## Scene 2 - The 2022 Firewall

Translate the BIS architecture into plain language: chips, systems, software,
technology, supercomputer end use, semiconductor manufacturing end use,
foreign-made items, and U.S.-person support. This is where law reaches through a
global supply chain. Then add the enforcement reality: a firewall creates a
market for diversion, shell companies, and third-country routing, which is why
BIS guidance later talks about diversion tactics.

## Scene 3 - Thresholds That Move

The controls are not one event. BIS revises; the Netherlands and Japan add their
own SME controls; companies adapt; BIS revises again. NVIDIA's H20 disclosure
gives the scene a concrete business consequence without turning the chapter into
a stock-market story.

## Scene 4 - Machines Behind The Machines

Move from accelerators to the machines and manufacturing stack that create them.
ASML belongs in the SME/lithography lane; TSMC belongs in the foundry and
advanced-packaging lane. EUV, DUV immersion, advanced nodes, CoWoS, HBM, and
software tools form the deeper choke points.

## Scene 5 - Rebuilding Capacity At State Speed

CHIPS is the counterweight: funding, incentives, packaging, workforce, and fabs.
But the pace is industrial, not software-fast. TSMC Arizona is useful here
because it is both a success story and a reminder of time, geography, and
dependency.

## Scene 6 - China's Counter-Levers

Use the MOFCOM/CSET translation to show the other side of supply-chain power:
materials controls. Keep the scene precise: these are not EUV machines, but they
are pressure points in a semiconductor-adjacent stack.

## Scene 7 - The Policy Swings

The AI Diffusion rule and rescission show that even the controlling state is
searching for the right shape of control. The story is not "more restriction
forever"; it is a struggle to balance adversary denial, allied access, domestic
industry, and global AI demand.

## Scene 8 - Capability Is Now A Treaty With Reality

Close with the new equation: model capability depends on chips, but chips depend
on export licenses, allied governments, foundries, packaging, memory, materials,
and physical sites. Ch72 picks up that last piece.

# Brief: Chapter 1 - The Laws of Thought

## Thesis
Before AI could be engineered, human reasoning had to be formalized into binary algebra. George Boole proved that logic is mechanical, not mystical. Crucially, Boole’s algebra was a class algebra not designed with physical computing or modern propositional logic in mind.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: George Boole's life and death, the 1854 publication of *The Laws of Thought*, the reduction of logic to binary states, the NOT operation (1-x), class algebra vs propositional logic, Mary Everest Boole's preservation of his work.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Physical implementation of logic (belongs to later chapters).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Self-Taught Mathematician:** Boole observes the messy nature of human arguments.
2. **The Class Algebra:** Exploring Boole's actual system, which was defined for mutually exclusive classes rather than modern inclusive-OR propositional logic.
3. **The Binary Reduction:** Boole formulates AND, mutually-exclusive addition, and NOT (1-x).
4. **The Legacy:** Boole's death in 1864 and Mary Everest Boole's role in keeping his work alive.
5. **The Dormant Blueprint:** Boole's algebra lies dormant for decades, viewed as elegant but practically useless by engineers.

# Scene Sketches: Chapter 1

## Scene 1: The Self-Taught Mathematician
- **Setting:** Queen's College, Cork, Ireland (1854).
- **Action:** Boole observes the messy nature of human arguments and seeks mathematical purity.

## Scene 2: The Class Algebra
- **Action:** Exploring Boole's actual system, pointing out it was defined for mutually exclusive classes rather than modern inclusive-OR propositional logic.

## Scene 3: The Binary Reduction
- **Action:** Pedagogical explanation of Boole's insight: replacing words with symbols and logic with math, reducing the universe to 1 and 0, and the introduction of NOT via (1-x).

## Scene 4: The Legacy
- **Action:** Boole's untimely death in 1864 and Mary Everest Boole's dedicated effort to preserve and teach his mathematical legacy.

## Scene 5: The Dormant Blueprint
- **Action:** A short, cautious coda noting that while Boole's algebra was mathematically profound, there is currently limited primary evidence of its practical engineering uptake during his era.

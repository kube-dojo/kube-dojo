# People: Chapter 50

## Verified Protagonists
- **Ashish Vaswani:** Transformer co-author. The paper's equal-contribution footnote says Ashish, with Illia Polosukhin, designed and implemented the first Transformer models and was involved in every aspect of the work.
- **Noam Shazeer:** Transformer co-author. The footnote credits him with scaled dot-product attention, multi-head attention, and the parameter-free position representation.
- **Niki Parmar:** Transformer co-author. The footnote credits her with designing, implementing, tuning, and evaluating many model variants in the original codebase and Tensor2Tensor.
- **Jakob Uszkoreit:** Transformer co-author. The footnote credits him with proposing the replacement of RNNs with self-attention and starting the evaluation effort.
- **Llion Jones:** Transformer co-author. The footnote credits him with model variants, the initial codebase, efficient inference, and visualizations.
- **Aidan N. Gomez:** Transformer co-author. The footnote credits him, with Lukasz Kaiser, with long implementation work on Tensor2Tensor; the title page says the work was performed while he was at Google Brain.
- **Lukasz Kaiser:** Transformer co-author. The footnote credits him, with Aidan Gomez, with implementation work on Tensor2Tensor that improved results and accelerated research.
- **Illia Polosukhin:** Transformer co-author. The footnote says he worked with Ashish Vaswani on designing and implementing the first Transformer models; the title page says the work was performed while he was at Google Research.

## Predecessor Figures

- **Ilya Sutskever, Oriol Vinyals, Quoc V. Le:** Authors of the 2014 sequence-to-sequence LSTM paper that provides the immediate recurrent encoder-decoder baseline.
- **Dzmitry Bahdanau, Kyunghyun Cho, Yoshua Bengio:** Authors of the attention/alignment paper that provides the immediate attention-before-Transformer bridge.

# People: Chapter 44 — The Latent Space

## Core Actors

- **Zellig S. Harris** — Linguist whose 1954 "Distributional Structure" supplies the chapter's pre-neural lineage: language can be described through occurrence relations and environments. Anchor: `sources.md` S1, pp.146-147.
- **Scott Deerwester, Susan T. Dumais, George W. Furnas, Thomas K. Landauer, Richard Harshman** — Authors of the 1990 LSA paper. Use them collectively as the latent-semantic-analysis bridge, not as Word2Vec protagonists. Anchor: `sources.md` S2, p.391.
- **Yoshua Bengio, Rejean Ducharme, Pascal Vincent, Christian Jauvin** — Authors of the 2003 neural probabilistic language model. Their work anchors learned distributed word feature vectors and the computational bottleneck Word2Vec later simplified. Anchor: `sources.md` S3, pp.1137, 1139, 1141-1142, 1148-1149.
- **Tomas Mikolov** — Lead author across the 2013 Word2Vec-related papers. The contract can say he was affiliated with Google Inc. for "Efficient Estimation" and "Distributed Representations"; "Linguistic Regularities" lists Microsoft Research with a note that he was currently at Google. Anchors: `sources.md` S4 p.1; S5 p.746; S6 p.1.
- **Kai Chen, Greg Corrado, Jeffrey Dean** — Google coauthors on "Efficient Estimation" and "Distributed Representations." Their role should be tied to the papers' architectures and infrastructure evidence, not invented internal lab scenes. Anchors: `sources.md` S4 p.1; S6 p.1.
- **Ilya Sutskever** — Google coauthor on the NIPS 2013 optimization/phrase paper. Anchor him only in Scene 5 unless more evidence is added. Anchor: `sources.md` S6 p.1.
- **Wen-tau Yih and Geoffrey Zweig** — Microsoft Research coauthors on "Linguistic Regularities," the analogy/vector-offset paper. Anchor: `sources.md` S5 p.746.

## Supporting Actors And References

- **Dan Jurafsky and James H. Martin** — Secondary synthesis source for distributional hypothesis, vector semantics, SGNS explanation, analogy caveats, and the static/contextual boundary. Anchor: `sources.md` S7, pp.1, 5-18, 22.
- **Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams** — Cited in S6 as an early distributed-representation antecedent from 1986; do not make them scene protagonists unless the original source is extracted. Anchor: `sources.md` S6 p.1.
- **Morin and Bengio; Mnih and Hinton; Collobert and Weston; Turian et al.** — Appear as technical predecessors/comparisons in S4-S6. Keep them in explanatory footwork, not narrative focus, unless additional anchors are pulled.

## Characterization Rules

- Do not infer motives for Mikolov or Google beyond what the papers say: reducing computational complexity, learning high-quality word vectors from huge corpora, and evaluating syntactic/semantic regularities.
- Do not write dialogue, lab atmosphere, or "moment of discovery" prose for the analogy examples. No primary source anchors that.
- Use affiliations exactly as printed in papers when needed; do not extrapolate team structure from author order.

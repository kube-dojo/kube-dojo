# Timeline: Chapter 44 — The Latent Space

- **1954:** Zellig S. Harris publishes "Distributional Structure" in *WORD*, framing language description in terms of relative occurrence/distribution and defining an element's distribution through its environments. Anchor: `sources.md` S1, pp.146-147.
- **1990:** Deerwester, Dumais, Furnas, Landauer, and Harshman publish "Indexing by Latent Semantic Analysis," using SVD to reduce term-document matrices into latent semantic factor spaces. Anchor: `sources.md` S2, p.391.
- **2003:** Bengio, Ducharme, Vincent, and Jauvin publish "A Neural Probabilistic Language Model" in *JMLR*, proposing learned distributed word feature vectors and emphasizing the curse of dimensionality in statistical language modeling. Anchor: `sources.md` S3, pp.1137, 1139.
- **2003:** Bengio et al. report that AP News experiments ran only five epochs and took about three weeks on 40 CPUs, marking the computational cost of early neural language models. Anchor: `sources.md` S3, pp.1148-1149.
- **January 2013:** Mikolov, Chen, Corrado, and Dean circulate arXiv:1301.3781, "Efficient Estimation of Word Representations in Vector Space," introducing CBOW and Skip-gram as efficient architectures for continuous word vectors. Anchor: `sources.md` S4, pp.1, 3-4.
- **June 9-14, 2013:** Mikolov, Yih, and Zweig's "Linguistic Regularities in Continuous Space Word Representations" appears at NAACL-HLT 2013 in Atlanta, presenting vector-offset analogy evaluation. Anchor: `sources.md` S5, p.746.
- **September 7, 2013:** arXiv:1301.3781v3 is available with expanded experimental details, including Google News 6B-token training, one-CPU training tables, and DistBelief experiments. Anchor: `sources.md` S4, pp.6-8.
- **2013:** Mikolov, Sutskever, Chen, Corrado, and Dean publish "Distributed Representations of Words and Phrases and their Compositionality" at NIPS, adding negative sampling, frequent-word subsampling, and phrase vectors. Anchor: `sources.md` S6, pp.1-5.
- **2025 secondary synthesis:** Jurafsky and Martin's Chapter 5 presents word2vec as static embeddings and distinguishes them from later contextual embeddings. Anchor: `sources.md` S7, pp.10-11.

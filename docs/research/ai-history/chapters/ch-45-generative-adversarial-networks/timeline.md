# Timeline: Chapter 45 - Generative Adversarial Networks

- **1992:** Jurgen Schmidhuber publishes "Learning Factorial Codes by Predictability Minimization" in *Neural Computation* 4(6):863-879. The 2014 GAN paper later names predictability minimization as the most relevant prior work involving competing neural networks, while distinguishing GANs from it.
- **2013:** Goodfellow receives a Google Fellowship in Deep Learning, noted in the 2014 paper's acknowledgments.
- **2014:** A later MIT Technology Review profile reports Goodfellow's GAN idea emerging during a night at Les 3 Brasseurs in Montreal; the 2014 paper acknowledges Les Trois Brasseurs for stimulating creativity.
- **2014:** Goodfellow, Pouget-Abadie, Mirza, Xu, Warde-Farley, Ozair, Courville, and Bengio publish "Generative Adversarial Nets" in NeurIPS 27.
- **2014:** The original experiments train adversarial nets on MNIST, Toronto Face Database, and CIFAR-10, using Theano/Pylearn2-era infrastructure and Compute Canada/Calcul Quebec support.
- **2015-2016:** Radford, Metz, and Chintala release DCGAN, introducing convolutional architecture constraints and showing latent-vector arithmetic in generated images; the arXiv v2 is dated January 7, 2016.
- **December 2016:** Goodfellow presents the NeurIPS 2016 GAN tutorial, later published as arXiv:1701.00160v4 on April 3, 2017.
- **October 2017 / April 2018:** Karras, Aila, Laine, and Lehtinen release Progressive Growing of GANs; NVIDIA lists it as ICLR 2018 with publication date April 30, 2018.
- **December 2018 / June 2019:** Karras, Laine, and Aila release StyleGAN as arXiv:1812.04948; NVIDIA lists it as IEEE CVPR 2019 with publication date June 16, 2019.
- **2020:** NVIDIA publishes StyleGAN2, revising artifacts and generator behavior; this belongs to boundary/context unless the human editor wants an extended epilogue.

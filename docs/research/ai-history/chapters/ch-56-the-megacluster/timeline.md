# Timeline: Chapter 56 - The Megacluster

- **March 11, 2019:** OpenAI announces OpenAI LP, a capped-profit structure designed to raise capital while keeping OpenAI Nonprofit in control and preserving the mission-first charter.
- **July 22, 2019:** Microsoft and OpenAI announce a multiyear, exclusive computing partnership with a $1 billion Microsoft investment, joint Azure AI supercomputing development, OpenAI services moving to Azure, and Microsoft as preferred commercialization partner.
- **February 13, 2020:** Microsoft Research announces Turing-NLG, a 17B-parameter language model, and describes the role of DeepSpeed, ZeRO, model parallelism, and multi-GPU training for very large models.
- **May 19, 2020:** Microsoft announces an Azure-hosted supercomputer built with and exclusively for OpenAI, described as one of the top five publicly disclosed supercomputers and published at more than 285,000 CPU cores, 10,000 GPUs, and 400 Gbps network connectivity per GPU server.
- **May 2020:** Microsoft frames the OpenAI supercomputer as part of AI at Scale: large models, training optimization tools, and supercomputing resources becoming available through Azure AI services and GitHub.

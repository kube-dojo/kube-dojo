# Brief: Chapter 2 - The Universal Machine

## Thesis
The concept of software was born from a mathematical puzzle. Turing's theoretical "Universal Machine" proved that a single machine could execute any computable logic, removing the need for specialized hardware for every task.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Hilbert and Ackermann's formulation of the *Entscheidungsproblem*, Turing's 1936 paper, the concept of the Turing Machine.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Bletchley Park.

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Unsolvable Problem (1928):** Hilbert poses the decision problem in Bologna, Italy.
2. **The Paper Tape (1936):** Turing conceptualizes a machine operating on an infinite tape.
3. **The Birth of Software:** Turing proves the machine's instructions can also be stored on the tape as data.

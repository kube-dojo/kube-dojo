# Brief: Chapter 2 - The Universal Machine

## Thesis
The concept of software was born from a mathematical puzzle. Turing's theoretical "Universal Machine" answered the Entscheidungsproblem by proving that a single machine could execute any computable logic, but he was preceded by Gödel's incompleteness theorem and paralleled by Church's lambda calculus.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Hilbert's *Entscheidungsproblem*, Gödel's 1931 incompleteness theorem, Alonzo Church's lambda calculus, Turing's 1936 paper, the Halting problem, the Turing-Church relationship at Princeton.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Bletchley Park codebreaking.

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Unsolvable Problem:** Hilbert poses the decision problem; Gödel shatters completeness and consistency in 1931.
2. **The Parallel Discovery:** Alonzo Church publishes the lambda calculus in early 1936, solving the decision problem mathematically.
3. **The Paper Tape:** Turing conceptualizes a machine operating on an infinite tape, making the Halting problem explicit.
4. **The Birth of Software:** Turing proves the machine's instructions can also be stored on the tape as data (the Universal Machine).
5. **The Princeton Connection:** Turing and Church's relationship and the formalization of the Church-Turing thesis.

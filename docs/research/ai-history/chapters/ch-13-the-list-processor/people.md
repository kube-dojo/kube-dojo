# People: Chapter 13 - The List Processor

## Main Figures

### John McCarthy (1927-2011)

- **Role:** LISP designer; author of the 1958 memo, 1960 CACM paper, and much of the LISP 1.5 manual; link between Dartmouth's AI naming agenda and MIT's symbolic-language infrastructure.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1958-1962 core LISP design and consolidation; 1978-1979 HOPL retrospective.
- **Primary source:** `AIM-001`, pp. 1-15; `Recursive60`; `LISP15`, p. 4; `HOPL-LISP`.
- **Drafting note:** Present McCarthy as language designer and theorist, not as sole implementer. `LISP15`, p. 4 makes the implementation collective.

### Steve Russell

- **Role:** Early LISP implementer; associated with the first practical `eval` interpreter realization.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1958-1959 early interpreter period; 1962 manual credit.
- **Primary source:** `LISP15`, p. 4 credits Russell and Edwards with programming the interpreter; `HOPL-LISP` gives McCarthy's later `eval` anecdote.
- **Drafting note:** The manual credit is Green. The "I can implement `eval`" realization should stay Yellow until paginated HOPL extraction.

### Daniel J. Edwards

- **Role:** Early interpreter implementer and LISP 1.5 manual co-author.
- **Dates for chapter:** early implementation period through 1962 manual.
- **Primary source:** `LISP15`, p. 4.
- **Drafting note:** Keep Edwards visible wherever the interpreter is discussed. Do not let the Russell anecdote erase the paired manual credit.

### Timothy P. Hart

- **Role:** LISP 1.5 manual co-author; compiler contributor; source of suggestions to the manual.
- **Dates for chapter:** LISP 1.5 consolidation period.
- **Primary source:** `LISP15`, p. 4.
- **Drafting note:** Hart belongs in the workbench/compiler/macro infrastructure layer, not as a main design protagonist unless more pages are extracted.

### Michael I. Levin

- **Role:** LISP 1.5 manual co-author; prepared the manual for publication; wrote Appendix B; compiler contributor.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1962 manual publication period.
- **Primary source:** `LISP15`, p. 4.
- **Drafting note:** Levin is important for the consolidation story: LISP becomes teachable and transferable partly through manualization.

### Marvin Minsky

- **Role:** MIT AI co-founder/context figure; contributed suggestions to the LISP 1.5 manual and helped program the compiler.
- **Dates for chapter:** MIT AI Project and LISP 1.5 period.
- **Primary source:** `LISP15`, p. 4; Ch11 for Dartmouth/MIT context.
- **Drafting note:** Use Minsky as institutional and implementation context, not as a replacement for McCarthy/Russell/Edwards.

### Paul W. Abrahams

- **Role:** LISP 1.5 manual co-author.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1962 manual.
- **Primary source:** `LISP15`, title page and p. 4.
- **Drafting note:** Include in the manual consolidation layer; do not invent a larger role without source pages.

### Allen Newell, J. C. Shaw, Herbert A. Simon

- **Role:** Ch12 cross-link; IPL/LT/GPS line proves that list-processing infrastructure predates LISP in AI.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1956-1959 as boundary context.
- **Primary source:** Ch12 `sources.md`; `HOPL-LISP` discussion of IPL.
- **Drafting note:** They should appear briefly to prevent a false-origin story. Ch13 should not retell LT/GPS.

## Review Notes

- The protagonist is a language system and research practice, not a single heroic author.
- Russell, Edwards, Hart, Levin, and Minsky are infrastructure figures. Their roles should be visible when the prose moves from theory to running system.
- If "first" appears in prose, verify what kind of first it is: first list-processing language is forbidden; first `eval` interpreter is currently Yellow; first AI language should be phrased cautiously.

# People: Chapter 49 - The Custom Silicon

## Verified Actors

- **Norman P. Jouppi:** Google Fellow and first author of the 2017 TPU paper. Google's 2017 TPU explainer identifies him as tech lead for the TPU project and notes his earlier role as one of the principal MIPS architects. Use him as the named architectural lead, not as a viewpoint character unless quoting an anchored interview or Google-republished quote.
- **Cliff Young:** Google Brain software engineer, co-author of the 2017 TPU paper, and co-author with Kaz Sato of Google's May 2017 public TPU v1 explainer. Strong anchor for the educational systolic-array exposition.
- **David Patterson:** Co-author of the TPU paper, UC Berkeley computer architect, Google engineer, and later co-author with John Hennessy of the CACM domain-specific-architecture framing. Use him as the bridge between TPU v1 and the broader architecture argument.
- **Jeff Dean:** Co-author of the TPU paper; Google research leader; co-author with Urs Hoelzle of the May 2017 Cloud TPU announcement. Use him in the closing hinge from internal inference ASIC to Cloud TPU platform.
- **Urs Hoelzle:** Google Cloud infrastructure leader and co-author of the May 2017 Cloud TPU announcement. Use him for the cloud/platform framing, not for TPU v1 design details.
- **Kaz Sato:** Google Cloud developer advocate and co-author of the May 2017 in-depth TPU explainer. Use as an authorial anchor for the public pedagogical explanation, not as a central historical protagonist.
- **John Hennessy:** Co-author with Patterson of the 2019 CACM domain-specific-architecture article; useful for the architecture-history close.

## Collective Actors

- **Google TPU team:** The 2017 paper has 75 named authors. Treat the chip as a large engineering effort, not as a single-inventor story. If prose needs a named center, use Jouppi for architecture/project leadership and Patterson/Young for technical exposition.
- **Google application teams:** The paper says six production inference workloads represented 95% of the TPU inference workload in July 2016. The chapter may mention workload classes and examples from Table 1, but should not assign every anonymized benchmark to a product.
- **Google Cloud / TensorFlow ecosystem:** The 2017 Cloud TPU announcement moves the story from internal infrastructure to externally available accelerator service and research access.

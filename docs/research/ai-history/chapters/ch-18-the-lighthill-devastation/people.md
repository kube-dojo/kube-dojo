# People: Chapter 18 - The Lighthill Devastation

- Sir James Lighthill: Cambridge applied mathematician and report author. Treat
  him as an outsider reviewer with a coherent practical-problem-solving
  philosophy, not as a cartoon anti-AI figure.
- N. S. Sutherland: Sussex experimental psychologist; defended the central
  category as Basic AI and argued that Lighthill's definition of B was
  misleading.
- R. M. Needham: Cambridge computer scientist; largely agreed with Lighthill's
  conclusions while adding nuance about heuristics, programming technology, and
  category-B justifications.
- H. C. Longuet-Higgins: Edinburgh theoretical psychologist and response author;
  useful as a middle voice if prose needs more than Sutherland/Michie.
- Donald Michie: Edinburgh machine intelligence leader; challenged Lighthill's
  classification and survey method, defended intelligence theory, and made the
  infrastructure argument for importing DEC System 10/PDP-10 machines.
- John McCarthy: US AI founder and BBC debate participant; do not attribute
  debate claims until the video has been watched/logged.
- Richard Gregory: psychologist and BBC debate participant; same debate
  guardrail as McCarthy.
- Terry Winograd: author of SHRDLU/*Understanding Natural Language*; appears in
  Lighthill's report as the remarkable table-top-world example that still leaves
  the wider-domain problem unresolved.
- Alan Bundy: Edinburgh AI researcher whose later personal account is useful for
  atmosphere and memory, but not as a sole factual anchor for policy mechanics.
- Jon Agar: historian of science; useful for interpreting Lighthill's practical
  problem-solving orientation and later Alvey/IKBS context.

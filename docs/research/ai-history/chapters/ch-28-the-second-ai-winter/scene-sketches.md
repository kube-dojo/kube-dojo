# Scene Sketches: Chapter 28 - The Second AI Winter

## Scene 1: The Successful Warning Sign

Open with XCON because it prevents a lazy "expert systems never worked" story. XCON configured DEC computer orders and became a celebrated industrial expert system, but Neumann's 1988 analysis shows the maintenance cost of scale: more than 6,200 rules, ~20,000 parts, about half the rules expected to change annually, and increasing difficulty ensuring correct rule firing order.

Evidence anchors:
- Neumann 1988 pp.34-37.

## Scene 2: The Knowledge Acquisition Bottleneck

Move from rule maintenance to the upstream bottleneck: how do experts communicate tacit heuristics to knowledge engineers? Atkinson's IAAI paper and the NASA/JSC report both preserve period optimism while admitting that shells/tools did not remove the hard human transfer problem.

Evidence anchors:
- Rajaram 1987 p.26-14.
- Atkinson 1990 p.161.

## Scene 3: The Demonstration Trap

Use DARPA Strategic Computing as the institutional scene. The issue was not that AI had no demos; it was that demos did not reliably transition into scalable, maintainable systems. Schwartz's critique that algorithms did not scale and that "new ideas" could not be bought by crash program provides the winter logic.

Evidence anchors:
- Roland/Shiman pp.274-276 for Schwartz's review and budget cuts.
- Roland/Shiman pp.279-281 for congressional pressure and producer-consumer/maturity-model thinking.

## Scene 4: The Fifth Generation Shadow

Japan's FGCS effort should appear as an expectation amplifier, not as a caricature. The final 1992 proceedings describe a ten-year project around knowledge processing, KL1, PIM, logic programming, and parallel inference. The narrative should contrast the ambition with the narrower research/prototype character of the final results.

Evidence anchors:
- Roland/Shiman pp.5, 37, 56-57 for FGCS as U.S./Western concern and Strategic Computing political leverage.
- FGCS 1992 pp.iii-v.

## Scene 5: Winter as Reallocation

Before the final handoff, include a short hardware-economics scene: Symbolics, once treated as an emblem of commercial AI hardware, was laying off workers and losing money while cheaper Sun/Apollo workstations eroded the specialized-machine moat. Keep this as one concrete business signal, not a full company history.

Evidence anchors:
- Bates 1988, LA Times.

## Scene 6: Winter as Reallocation

Close by showing resources and prestige moving to architectures, high-performance computing, benchmarks, speech, machine learning, and statistical methods. This hands off to SVMs, statistical speech, and reinforcement learning without making Ch28 a despair chapter.

Evidence anchors:
- Roland/Shiman p.285 for SC disappearing into HPC.
- Roland/Shiman pp.274-276 for selective support of speech/NavLab/neural modeling/machine learning.

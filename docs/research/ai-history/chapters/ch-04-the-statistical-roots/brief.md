# Brief: Chapter 4 - The Statistical Roots

## Thesis
Andrey Markov's mathematical extension of the Law of Large Numbers to dependent variables, and his subsequent analysis of *Eugene Onegin*, laid the distant statistical groundwork for modern predictive text generation.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: The Nekrasov-Markov theological dispute over free will, Markov's 1906 mathematical proof, his 1913 analysis of Pushkin including 2-step (second-order) chains, Shannon's 1948 citation of Markov.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Claims that Markov foresaw modern AI or LLMs.

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Theological Dispute:** Nekrasov argues free will requires independence; Markov counters using probability.
2. **The 1906 Proof:** Markov mathematically extends the Law of Large Numbers to dependent variables.
3. **Counting Pushkin (1913):** Markov applies his proof to *Eugene Onegin*, demonstrating first-order and 2-step (second-order) chains.
4. **The Ancestry of Generation:** Claude Shannon cites Markov in 1948 for English n-grams, bridging the concept to information theory.

## Conflict Notes

- **Markov's retirement year:** Hayes 2013 ("Peterburgian Math") says Markov "had already retired" by 1906 at age 50. Seneta 2006 p.2 says he retired "as Emeritus Professor in 1905." Reconcilable: 1905 from St. Petersburg University; he remained Academician at the Imperial Academy of Sciences. Use 1905 as the retirement year and clarify "from St. Petersburg University" if needed.
- **Vowel/consonant pair counts:** The primary Markov 1913 (Link translation) p.596 works with 11,361 (= 11,362 − 1 for the unpredecessored first letter) for the digram denominator. Hayes 2013 uses 11,362 as the unconditional consonant count. Both are correct in their context. Do not let prose mix the two figures without flagging the technical reason.
- **"Free will" vs. "voluntary action":** Hayes paraphrases Nekrasov as making a free-will argument; Seneta is more careful, framing it as Markov's interpretation that Nekrasov had argued pairwise independence was necessary as well as sufficient for the WLLN. Use Seneta's framing for the technical mathematical claim; use Hayes's framing for the theological context. They are compatible.
- **Shannon's Markov citation:** Shannon 1948 footnote 6 cites Fréchet 1938, not Markov 1906 or 1913. Treat the Markov→Shannon link as concept-transmission via the established mathematical literature (Markov-process theory had been generalized through Kolmogorov, Khinchin, Fréchet, Doob), not as direct citation. Do not draft a scene where Shannon reads Markov.

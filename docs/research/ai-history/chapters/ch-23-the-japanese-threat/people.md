# People: Chapter 23 - The Japanese Threat

## Core Cast

- **Kazuhiro Fuchi:** ICOT Research Center director and primary voice for the
  project's philosophy. Use his 1984 keynote for the original predicate-logic /
  kernel-language frame and his 1992 retrospective for the exaggerated public
  image and narrowed actual goals.
- **H. Kinoshita:** Director-General of MITI's Machinery and Information
  Industries Bureau. Use as the policy voice connecting FGCS to Japan's advanced
  information society, semiconductor progress, and software crisis.
- **Koichi Furukawa:** ICOT researcher and TR-228 author. Use for the
  mid-project technical frame: knowledge information processing, logic
  programming, highly parallel architecture, GHC/CIL, and program transformation.
- **Takashi Kurozumi:** ICOT author of the 1992 ten-year overview. Use for the
  project chronology, stages, budgets, PSI/PIM summary, and relationship between
  ICOT and related organizations.
- **Shunichi Uchida:** ICOT author/editorial voice in the final evaluation and
  follow-on project. Use for software disclosure, follow-on dissemination, KLIC,
  and the transition from PIM-only software to Unix-based environments.
- **H. Gallaire:** External evaluator in the 1992 workshop. Use for balanced
  critique: limited real-world applications and manufacturer impact, but strong
  technical achievement and useful skills.
- **T. Moto-oka:** Conference/project figure and editor of the 1982 FGCS
  proceedings. Current contract uses him as institutional context only unless a
  direct source is added.
- **Kazunori Ueda / Chikayama:** Important KL1/GHC figures mentioned in Fuchi
  and Kurozumi. Use only as technical contributors if prose needs names inside
  the language story.

## Institutional Cast

- **MITI:** Japanese Ministry of International Trade and Industry; project
  sponsor and industrial-policy actor. Distinguish MITI's policy goals from
  ICOT's technical work.
- **ICOT:** Institute for New Generation Computer Technology; the central
  research institute created to promote the FGCS project.
- **Japanese computer manufacturers:** Participants, subcontractors, and parent
  companies for rotating researchers. Do not treat them as a single unified
  narrator without source support.
- **U.S. OTA/NRC observers:** Use for the Western "threat" frame and later
  assessment, not as neutral descriptions of Japanese intentions.
- **DARPA:** Relevant through Strategic Computing only if sourced. Current
  contract has NRC and NTRL anchors but not a parsed DARPA plan.

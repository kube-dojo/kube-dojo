# Gap Analysis: Chapter 23 - The Japanese Threat

## Current Verdict

`REVIEW_REQUESTED`

The chapter has enough anchored evidence for a 4,000-5,000 word draft if it
keeps the Western "threat" narrative tied to verified perception sources and
keeps the Japanese side grounded in ICOT/MITI technical documents. It should not
stretch into a full DARPA/Alvey/MCC response chapter until those primary sources
are parsed.

Word Count Discipline label: `4k-5k confirmed; 5k-5.7k requires Western-response unlocks`

## Green Scenes

- **Conference warning:** Fuchi 1984/1992 and OTA 1983 support the worldwide
  sensation and Western competitive reading.
- **Software crisis:** Kinoshita 1984 and Furukawa 1987 support domestic
  computerization, software shortage, software productivity, and knowledge
  information processing as a target market.
- **Technical stack:** Furukawa 1987, Fuchi 1984, and Kurozumi 1992 support
  logic programming as the bridge between knowledge processing and parallel
  architecture.
- **National research organization:** Kurozumi 1992 supports 1979-1981 study,
  1982 launch, ICOT, stages, budgets, and 11-year extension.
- **Inference machines:** Fuchi 1984/1992 and Kurozumi 1992 support PSI, ESP,
  SIMPOS, GHC, KL1, Multi-PSI, PIM, PIMOS, and selected application systems.
- **Western threat perception:** OTA 1983 supports U.S. period fear of Japanese
  leapfrogging and software lock-in; NRC 2005 supports the later conclusion that
  FGCS helped stimulate DARPA Strategic Computing while posing little direct
  company threat.
- **Mixed evaluation:** Fuchi 1992, FinalEval p.4, Gallaire 1992, and Uchida
  1994 support a non-binary close: exaggerated image, real prototype work,
  limited application/manufacturer impact, free software, and portability work.

## Yellow / Thin Areas

- **DARPA primary detail:** NTRL confirms the Strategic Computing plan metadata
  and abstract, but the full plan PDF was not parsed locally. Keep DARPA compact
  unless a source is added.
- **Alvey and MCC:** Current contract does not parse primary sources. They can
  appear as response examples only if clearly caveated or omitted.
- **Original 1982/1983 ICOT Outline:** Identified through Stanford/AIST-related
  discovery, but not parsed in this contract. Later ICOT retrospectives cover
  most core project facts.
- **Budget comparisons:** Kurozumi's 54 billion yen final figure and OTA's $500
  million period projection are both sourced, but should not be casually
  converted or reconciled without additional context.
- **Foreign press panic:** The chapter has institutional U.S. analysis, not a
  broad press archive. Avoid claims about "the media" unless tied to Fuchi's
  statement about sensational news.

## Red / Excluded Claims

- "FGCS was a total failure."
- "FGCS created human-level machine translation."
- "FGCS was just Prolog hype."
- "FGCS alone caused DARPA Strategic Computing, Alvey, MCC, or the AI winter."
- "Japan was going to take over AI" as authorial voice.
- Exact Alvey, DARPA, or MCC budgets without parsed primary sources.
- "MITI, ICOT, and Japanese manufacturers all wanted the same thing" without
  source support.

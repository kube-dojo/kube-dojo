# Timeline: Chapter 29 - Support Vector Machines

- **1964:** Aizerman, Braverman, and Rozonoer publish potential-function work later cited in Cortes/Vapnik as kernel lineage.
- **1971:** Vapnik and Chervonenkis publish uniform-convergence theory, giving later statistical-learning prose a concrete source for capacity/generalization rather than just a retrospective label.
- **1982:** Vapnik's *Estimation of Dependences Based on Empirical Data* consolidates optimal-hyperplane/statistical-learning foundations later cited by Cortes/Vapnik.
- **1992:** Boser, Guyon, and Vapnik publish the optimal-margin classifier training algorithm at COLT, bringing maximum margins and kernels together for practical classifiers.
- **1993-1995:** Cortes and Vapnik develop/publish support-vector networks, including soft margins for non-separable data.
- **1994-1995:** OCR comparisons and the Cortes/Vapnik paper place SVMs on USPS/NIST handwritten-digit benchmarks next to LeNet and classical classifiers.
- **1998:** Burges publishes a widely used SVM tutorial, evidence that the method had become important enough to need careful transmission to the pattern-recognition community.

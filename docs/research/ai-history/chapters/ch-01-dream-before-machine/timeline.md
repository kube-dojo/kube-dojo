# Timeline: Chapter 1

- **1943:** McCulloch and Pitts publish "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity", inspiring both cybernetics and later neural networks.
- **1945:** John von Neumann drafts the EDVAC report, defining the stored-program digital computer.
- **1946-1953:** The Macy Conferences on Cybernetics take place, dominated by analog and biological analogies.
- **1948:** Norbert Wiener publishes "Cybernetics", formalizing the field.
- **1953:** W. Grey Walter publishes "The Living Brain", detailing his analog tortoises (Machina speculatrix).
- **August 31, 1955:** McCarthy, Minsky, Rochester, and Shannon submit the proposal for the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence.
- **1956:** The IBM 704 is introduced, becoming the primary infrastructure for early AI research.

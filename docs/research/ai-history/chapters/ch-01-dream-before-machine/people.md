# People: Chapter 1

## Verified Protagonists

- **Norbert Wiener:** The father of Cybernetics. His vision was heavily biological and analog, focusing on feedback loops. He represented the old guard that McCarthy wanted to break away from.
- **John von Neumann:** Mathematician and polymath. His conceptualization of the stored-program computer (von Neumann architecture) provided the digital infrastructure that made symbolic AI possible.
- **W. Grey Walter:** Neurophysiologist and roboticist. Built some of the first electronic autonomous robots (the "tortoises") using analog circuitry, demonstrating the physical limitations of non-digital hardware.
- **John McCarthy:** The primary driver behind the Dartmouth conference. He coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" explicitly to distinguish the field from Cybernetics and to focus on digital, symbolic logic.
- **Nathaniel Rochester:** Designer of the IBM 701 (IBM's first commercial scientific computer). His presence at Dartmouth provided the crucial corporate and infrastructural backing (IBM 704 time) needed for the new field.

### Primary
- [x] **"A Proposal for the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence" (McCarthy, Minsky, Rochester, Shannon, August 31, 1955)**
  - Verification: Green. Establishes the foundational definition and explicitly requests time on the IBM 704, anchoring Scene 3.
- [x] **"Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine" (Norbert Wiener, 1948)**
  - Verification: Green. The foundational text for the analog/biological approach that preceded symbolic AI, anchoring Scene 1.
- [x] **"First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC" (John von Neumann, 1945)**
  - Verification: Green. The primary architectural document defining the stored-program computer concept that enabled symbolic AI, anchoring Scene 2.

  - Verification: Green. Primary account of early analog robotic experiments (tortoises) constrained by physical wiring, anchoring Scene 1.
- [x] **"IBM Electronic Data-Processing Machines Type 704 Manual of Operation" (IBM, 1956)**
  - Verification: Green. Primary infrastructural document detailing the capabilities that the Dartmouth organizers relied upon, anchoring Scene 3.
- [x] **"The Logic Theory Machine: A Complex Information Processing System" (Allen Newell, Herbert A. Simon, 1956)**
  - Verification: Green. Proves they had a working program at Dartmouth and establishes their rejection of the "AI" label, anchoring Scene 4.

### Secondary
- [x] **"The Cybernetics Moment: Or Why We Call Our Age the Information Age" (Ronald R. Kline, 2015)**
  - Verification: Green. Provides historical context on the Macy Conferences and the infrastructural/ideological clash between cybernetics and digital AI, anchoring Scene 1.
- [x] **"Machines Who Think: A Personal Inquiry into the History and Prospects of Artificial Intelligence" (Pamela McCorduck, 1979/2004)**
  - Verification: Green. Comprehensive secondary source detailing the transition from early cybernetics to the Dartmouth workshop and the von Neumann shift, anchoring Scenes 2 and 3.
- [x] **"Turing's Cathedral: The Origins of the Digital Universe" (George Dyson, 2012)**
  - Verification: Green. Details the transition to the von Neumann architecture and stored-program computing at the IAS, anchoring Scene 2.
- [x] **"Mind as Machine: A History of Cognitive Science" (Margaret A. Boden, 2006)**
  - Verification: Green. Provides rigorous historical context on the Macy Conferences, detailing the interdisciplinary debates between cyberneticists like Wiener and anthropologists like Mead and Bateson.
- [x] **"Artificial Intelligence: A Guide for Thinking Humans" (Melanie Mitchell, 2019)**
  - Verification: Green. Provides historical details on the chaotic reality of the Dartmouth conference, anchoring Scene 4.

## Conflict Notes
- **Cybernetics vs. AI split:** McCorduck portrays the split between Cybernetics and AI as a natural evolution of ideas, while Kline emphasizes it as a deliberate boundary-drawing exercise by McCarthy to distance the new field from Wiener's established discipline. The narrative will lean towards the infrastructure-driven split: McCarthy's camp recognized that digital mainframes offered a fundamentally different, symbolic capability than analog cybernetics.

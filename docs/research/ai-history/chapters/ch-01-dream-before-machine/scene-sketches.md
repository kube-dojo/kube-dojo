# Scene Sketches: Chapter 1

## Scene 1: The Tortoise and the Vacuum Tube
- **Setting:** W. Grey Walter's lab.
- **Action:** Describe the fascinating but ultimately limited behavior of the analog tortoises. They could navigate and find light, but they couldn't "think" in symbols.
- **Citation Anchor:** Walter (1953).
- **Infrastructural Point:** Biology is an inspiring model, but mimicking it with vacuum tubes and analog circuits hits a hard physical wall.

## Scene 1a: The Macy Conferences
- **Setting:** The Beekman Hotel, New York City (1946-1953).
- **Action:** Interdisciplinary debates with Wiener, Mead, Bateson, McCulloch, and Pitts. The obsession with homeostasis and biological feedback loops.
- **Citation Anchor:** Wiener (1948), Boden (2006).
- **Infrastructural Point:** The peak of the analog/biological view of intelligence before the digital revolution forced a paradigm split.

## Scene 2: The Stored Program
- **Setting:** Princeton / the drafting of the EDVAC report.
- **Action:** Von Neumann realizes that memory and instructions can be the same thing. The machine doesn't need to be rewired; it just needs new symbols.
- **Citation Anchor:** von Neumann (1945), Dyson (2012).
- **Infrastructural Point:** This shifts the bottleneck from hardware engineering (soldering) to software engineering (coding), providing the blank slate AI needed.

## Scene 3: The IBM Gambit
- **Setting:** 1955, drafting the Dartmouth Proposal.
- **Action:** McCarthy wants to break away from Wiener's Cybernetics. He realizes that to do this, he needs raw digital power. He partners with Nathaniel Rochester of IBM.
- **Citation Anchor:** McCarthy et al. (1955), IBM (1956).
- **Infrastructural Point:** The definition of "Artificial Intelligence" wasn't just a philosophical choice; it was a bid for time on the new IBM 704 mainframes, tying the field's destiny to digital computing.

## Scene 4: The Reality of Dartmouth
- **Setting:** Dartmouth College, Summer 1956.
- **Action:** The contrast between the romantic legend and the messy reality. People coming and going, Shannon distracted. Newell and Simon arrive with a working program (*Logic Theorist*) running on the JOHNNIAC, act aloof, and reject the term "AI."
- **Citation Anchor:** Mitchell (2019), Newell & Simon (1956).
- **Infrastructural Point:** Shows that list-processing and symbolic logic (via JOHNNIAC) were already working in practice, proving the digital paradigm shift had successfully occurred, even if the attendees couldn't agree on the name.

# Brief: Chapter 62 - Multimodal Convergence

## Thesis
By the mid-2020s, "language model" was no longer a sufficient category. Models that began as text predictors became interfaces for images, audio, video, screen understanding, and cross-modal reasoning. This chapter should explain the convergence of text, vision, audio, and video as a product and infrastructure shift, while keeping source claims separate from demo hype.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: GPT-4V/GPT-4o-style multimodal interfaces, Gemini-style native multimodality claims, image/audio/video generation, Sora/Veo-type video systems, and the infrastructure implications of long context, high-bandwidth media, and multimodal evaluation.
- OUT OF SCOPE: complete film-industry history, every image/video model, and unsupported claims about world models or physical understanding.

## Required Scenes
1. **Language Model Becomes Interface:** Text remains the control surface, but inputs and outputs expand beyond text.
2. **Vision Enters The Loop:** Image understanding changes the product from chatbot to visual assistant.
3. **Audio And Real-Time Interaction:** Speech collapses the delay between model and user.
4. **Video As Scale Problem:** Generated video makes temporal coherence, compute, data rights, and evaluation harder.
5. **Category Collapse:** The chapter closes on why "LLM" becomes a misleading shorthand.

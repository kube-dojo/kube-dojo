# Brief: Chapter 67 - The Monopoly

## Thesis

The monopoly in late-era AI was not a single cartoon villain. It was a stack of
chokepoints: accelerator supply, cloud capacity, model partnerships, API
distribution, enterprise channels, safety/compliance infrastructure, and
frontier-lab capital needs. Open weights challenged closed labs, but they did
not dissolve platform power. This chapter should show how the industry rebuilt
itself around a few firms that could finance, host, distribute, and govern
frontier models, while regulators began asking whether partnerships could
entrench old digital-market power inside the new AI stack.

## Boundary Contract

- IN SCOPE: NVIDIA data-center demand as a compute chokepoint; Microsoft/OpenAI
  2019 exclusive cloud/provider arrangement and later 2025-2026 amendments;
  Amazon/Anthropic and Google/Anthropic cloud/compute partnerships; FTC 6(b)
  inquiry and 2025 staff report; July 2024 joint competition statement from
  US/EU/UK regulators; "monopoly" as stack concentration and bottleneck power,
  not a final legal conclusion.
- OUT OF SCOPE: benchmark politics (Ch66); open-weight licensing (Ch65);
  copyright/data-labor disputes (Ch68); export controls/chip geopolitics (Ch71);
  datacenter energy and grid build-out (Ch70/72); broad antitrust history beyond
  the AI-specific sources.
- Transition from Ch66: benchmarks decide reputation, but reputation only turns
  into market power when distribution, compute, cloud contracts, and enterprise
  channels can convert scores into products.
- Transition to Ch68/70/71/72: concentration raises the stakes of data,
  copyright, energy, and chip-geopolitics fights.

## Required Scenes

1. **The Stack Has More Than One Gate:** Define the monopoly as layered
   bottlenecks: chips, cloud, data, capital, distribution, and compliance.
2. **The GPU Tollbooth:** NVIDIA's fiscal 2024 data-center surge shows compute
   becoming the scarce input everyone had to route around or through.
3. **The Cloud Marriage:** Microsoft/OpenAI makes the template visible in 2019:
   investment, Azure supercomputing, exclusive cloud provision, and preferred
   commercialization.
4. **The Template Replicates:** Amazon/Anthropic and Google/Anthropic show
   frontier labs pairing model development with hyperscaler compute and custom
   chips.
5. **Regulators Name The Pattern:** FTC and joint US/EU/UK competition sources
   frame partnerships, key inputs, and incumbent market power as competition
   risks.
6. **Exclusivity Evolves Instead Of Disappearing:** Close with the 2025-2026
   Microsoft/OpenAI amendments: exclusivity relaxes, primary-cloud and IP
   advantages remain, showing that platform power mutates rather than vanishes.

## Guardrails

- Do not claim any company is legally a monopoly unless a cited legal finding
  says so. Use "concentration," "bottleneck," "chokepoint," and "platform power."
- Do not state Microsoft/OpenAI exclusivity as current without dating it. The
  2019 arrangement, 2025 revision, February 2026 clarification, and April 27,
  2026 amendment all need separate treatment.
- Do not treat company press releases as neutral market analysis. Use them for
  what the companies said the partnerships did.
- Do not import Ch70/72 power-grid material except as a handoff.
- Do not import Ch71 export-control geopolitics except to say chip access will
  be handled there.

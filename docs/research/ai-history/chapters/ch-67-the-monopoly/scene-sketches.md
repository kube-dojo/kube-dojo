# Scene Sketches: Chapter 67 - The Monopoly

## Scene 1: The Stack Has More Than One Gate

Open by rejecting the simple picture of a single AI monopolist. The regulators'
joint statement gives the cleaner frame: specialized chips, compute, data,
technical expertise, platforms, distribution channels, and partnerships can each
become gates. The prose should make the reader see a stack of toll booths rather
than one fortress.

Guardrail: do not use "monopoly" as legal conclusion. The title is narrative
pressure; the body must say "concentration" and "bottlenecks."

## Scene 2: The GPU Tollbooth

NVIDIA's 2024 10-K makes compute concrete: data-center revenue up 217%, cloud
providers representing more than half of Q4 data-center revenue, and AI inference
already a major share. This scene explains why the frontier race became a
procurement race before it became a product race.

Guardrail: do not turn this into export controls or chip geopolitics. Ch71 owns
that.

## Scene 3: The Cloud Marriage

Use OpenAI's 2019 announcement as the original template: a frontier lab needs
capital and compute; a hyperscaler needs frontier models and enterprise product
gravity. Azure supercomputing, exclusive cloud provider, and preferred
commercialization partner are the anchor phrases. The scene should show why this
was not merely funding; it tied research, infrastructure, and distribution.

Guardrail: date all exclusivity language to 2019 unless discussing later updates.

## Scene 4: The Template Replicates

Amazon/Anthropic and Google/Anthropic show the same structure with different
terms: cloud provider, training partner, custom accelerators, foundation-model
deployment, and safety/responsibility language. The point is not that every deal
is identical; the point is that frontier labs repeatedly pair with hyperscaler
capacity.

Guardrail: company announcements are self-descriptions. Do not infer hidden
market motives.

## Scene 5: Regulators Name The Pattern

FTC's 6(b) inquiry and staff report turn the private-deal story into public
market structure. The joint statement then names the wider concerns: restriction
of key inputs, incumbent power extending into AI, distribution channels, and
partnerships that could coopt threats. This scene should feel like the moment the
state starts mapping the AI stack.

Guardrail: regulators identified risks and information gaps; they did not issue
a final monopoly finding in these sources.

## Scene 6: Exclusivity Evolves Instead Of Disappearing

Close on the 2025-2026 Microsoft/OpenAI sequence. October 2025 preserved Azure
API/IP exclusivity while opening some doors. February 2026 reaffirmed Azure
exclusivity for stateless APIs. April 27, 2026 changed the shape again: primary
cloud remains, all-cloud serving becomes possible, and IP licensing becomes
non-exclusive through 2032. The ending should show mutating platform power: less
simple than lock-in, more durable than a clean break.

Guardrail: this scene is current as of April 28, 2026; if drafted later, recheck
the latest partnership terms.

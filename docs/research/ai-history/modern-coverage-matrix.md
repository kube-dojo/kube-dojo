# Modern Coverage Matrix

This matrix records the 2026-04-28 structural decision to expand the AI History roadmap from 68 to 72 chapters. Claude and Gemini both recommended adding chapters rather than forcing modern themes into unrelated hosts.

## Added Chapters

| Chapter | Theme | Why It Needs Its Own Slot |
|---|---|---|
| Ch60 - The Agent Turn | RAG, search, tool use, function calling, early agents | This is the bridge from chatbot products to operational systems; it would be buried inside Product Shock or Inference Economics. |
| Ch62 - Multimodal Convergence | vision, audio, video, real-time multimodal products | The category shift from text-only LLMs to multimodal systems is broader than diffusion and broader than scale. |
| Ch66 - Benchmark Wars | evaluations, leaderboards, contamination, product/policy claims | Modern evals become market and policy infrastructure, not only scientific measurement. |
| Ch68 - Data Labor and the Copyright Reckoning | RLHF labor, scraping, books, images, lawsuits, opt-outs | Labor and rights disputes are distinct from the technical data-exhaustion problem. |

## Guardrails

- Do not let added chapters drift into trend surveys. Each must retain the book's infrastructure-and-math thesis.
- Do not draft modern claims without fresh source verification; many late-era facts are temporally unstable.
- Do not treat marketing demos, benchmark tables, or legal complaints as stronger evidence than they are.
- Do not collapse social/labor/legal history into "data exhaustion"; that flattening was the gap this expansion fixes.

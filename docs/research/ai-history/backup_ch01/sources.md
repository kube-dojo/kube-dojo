# Sources: Chapter 1

## Annotated Bibliography

### Primary
- [x] **"An Investigation of the Laws of Thought on Which are Founded the Mathematical Theories of Logic and Probabilities" (George Boole, 1854)**
  - Verification: Green. The foundational text that reduced human logic to binary algebraic equations (1 and 0), anchoring Scene 1.
- [x] **"On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem" (Alan Turing, 1936)**
  - Verification: Green. The legendary paper introducing the theoretical "Universal Computing Machine" (Turing Machine), which proved that logic could be mechanized on an infinite tape, anchoring Scene 3.
- [x] **"A Symbolic Analysis of Relay and Switching Circuits" (Claude E. Shannon, 1937)**
  - Verification: Green. The seminal master's thesis proving that Boolean algebra perfectly models the behavior of electrical relay circuits, establishing the bridge between theoretical logic and physical hardware, anchoring Scene 4.

### Secondary
- [x] **"The Information: A History, a Theory, a Flood" (James Gleick, 2011)**
  - Verification: Green. Provides rigorous historical context on Shannon’s discovery of the link between Boole’s forgotten algebra and the messy physical routing switches of the Bell Telephone system, anchoring Scenes 1 and 4.
- [x] **"Alan Turing: The Enigma" (Andrew Hodges, 1983)**
  - Verification: Green. The definitive, deeply researched biography of Alan Turing. Covers the intellectual climate of Cambridge in the 1930s, his response to Hilbert’s *Entscheidungsproblem*, and his conceptualization of the Universal Machine, anchoring Scenes 2 and 3.

## Conflict Notes
- **Turing vs. Church:** Alonzo Church published his proof (Lambda Calculus) slightly before Turing published his (Turing Machines). While mathematically equivalent (the Church-Turing thesis), the narrative will focus heavily on Turing because the *Turing Machine* is an infrastructural metaphor (a tape, a read/write head, a machine state). It perfectly sets up the transition from theoretical math to physical hardware, whereas Church's proof was purely linguistic.

# Scene Sketches: Chapter 1

## Scene 1: The Laws of Thought
- **Setting:** Queen's College, Cork, Ireland (1854).
- **Action:** George Boole publishes his obscure text, arguing that the chaotic, mysterious process of human logic can be mathematically represented by just two symbols: 1 (True) and 0 (False).
- **Citation Anchor:** Boole (1854), Gleick (2011).
- **Infrastructural Point:** Establish the core thesis of the book—intelligence isn't magic; it's mechanizable logic. But in 1854, there is no hardware to run this logic on.

## Scene 2: Hilbert's Challenge
- **Setting:** Bologna, Germany (1928), the International Congress of Mathematicians.
- **Action:** David Hilbert throws down the gauntlet: The *Entscheidungsproblem*. He asks if mathematics itself can be automated by a mechanical process.
- **Citation Anchor:** Hodges (1983).
- **Infrastructural Point:** Set up the stakes. Mathematicians are looking for a theoretical machine before engineers are looking for a physical one.

## Scene 3: The Universal Machine
- **Setting:** King's College, Cambridge (1936).
- **Action:** Alan Turing, lying in a meadow, conceptualizes a scanner reading symbols off an infinite tape. He proves that this single "Universal Machine" can simulate the logic of any specialized machine.
- **Citation Anchor:** Turing (1936), Hodges (1983).
- **Infrastructural Point:** The ultimate software abstraction. Turing proves that hardware doesn't need to be specialized for every task; a general-purpose architecture is mathematically sound.

## Scene 4: The Bridge to Physics
- **Setting:** MIT, Cambridge, Massachusetts (1937).
- **Action:** Claude Shannon, working on the massive, clunky analog Differential Analyzer, realizes that the complex telephone relays he is studying perfectly execute Boolean algebra.
- **Citation Anchor:** Shannon (1937), Gleick (2011).
- **Infrastructural Point:** The climax of the chapter. Shannon proves that Boole's abstract 1s and 0s can be physically manifested as electrical switches (open/closed). Theoretical mathematics suddenly becomes an engineering discipline. The age of the physical machine has begun.
